Deuteronomy 31:6

NLT (New Living Translation)

6 So be strong and courageous! Do not be afraid and do not panic before them. For the Lord your God will personally go ahead of you. He will neither fail you nor abandon you.”


GUIDE REFLECTION:


Life often presents us with challenges that appear insurmountable. These moments can leave us feeling helpless and defeated, as if there is no possible way forward. However, for those who place their trust in God, there is a profound truth that brings hope and assurance: God makes a way through what seems impossible at first. This belief is rooted in biblical accounts and personal testimonies that demonstrate God's ability to intervene in miraculous ways, turning hopeless situations into triumphs.

If there are two things we, as humans, are well acquainted with, it's weakness and fear. We know what it feels like to be weak, and we know what it's like to stare fear in the face and desperately want to flee.

But praise be to God, fear and weakness aren’t part of our inheritance. The Bible repeatedly tells us that when it comes to fear, we should fear nothing more than God. This fear isn’t one of dread or anxiety but a reverent, awe-inspired fear driven by our desire to please Him. We understand that He is our strong tower, our protection, the one who fights for us. He created all things, so having Him on our side guarantees victory in every situation we encounter.



Where Should We Get Our Strength and Courage From?

Our strength and courage don't originate from a healthy diet, a specific spiritual plan, or a particular healing program. Genuine strength and courage come from God, through Christ, as we faithfully follow His words and purpose for our lives. The Bible assures us that when we seek Him with all our hearts, we will find Him. Spending time in prayer and studying God's Word helps us understand the next steps He has for us. Our strength derives from His power and truth flowing through our minds and hearts. God desires a relationship with us, but relationships are mutual. We must invest effort into this relationship by getting to know God through Scripture and applying what we learn from it.

Strength is about surrendering to our Lord Jesus and allowing God to work through the talents He has given us and the paths He has prepared. As Crawford Loritts preached, “God isn’t needy of worthy men; He makes men worthy.” Paul wrote about how God formed and purposed him in his mother’s womb. We can find courage in knowing that God knows who we are and who we were always meant to be. Everything He does and allows is designed to draw us closer to Him. Jon Bloom writes, “God does not want us to be strong; God wants to be our strength.” Courage comes from the essence of who He is and whose we are. Strength and courage come from a dedicated and obedient heart, through Christ.



God Calls Us to Live in His Strength, Not Ours

We have come to understand that God consistently calls us to tasks that seem beyond our capabilities. This is because, without Him, we cannot accomplish them. He calls us to show extraordinary strength and courage, knowing that these are not achievements we can attain on our own. God invites us to step out in faith and obedience, trusting that He will establish a firm path under our feet. We take the initial steps, but He is the one who performs the mighty and powerful work in our lives.



And Finally, God Makes a Way through What Seems Impossible at First

The Bible is replete with stories of individuals who faced seemingly impossible circumstances, yet witnessed God's miraculous intervention. One of the most compelling examples is the story of Moses and the Israelites at the Red Sea. Trapped between the pursuing Egyptian army and the vast expanse of water, the Israelites' situation seemed utterly hopeless. Yet, in their moment of despair, God commanded Moses to stretch out his hand over the sea, and the waters parted, allowing the Israelites to cross on dry ground. This miraculous event not only delivered them from imminent danger but also showcased God's power to make a way where there seemed to be none.

In the New Testament, we see the apostle Paul encountering numerous trials and persecutions. Imprisoned, shipwrecked, and beaten, Paul faced circumstances that would have broken many. However, his steadfast faith in God's providence enabled him to persevere. Paul's journey serves as a testament to God's ability to provide a way through adversity, transforming trials into opportunities for growth and witness.

These biblical narratives are not just ancient tales; they offer timeless lessons for our lives today. Many people have experienced God's intervention in their own seemingly impossible situations. Personal testimonies abound of individuals who have seen God make a way through financial crises, health challenges, broken relationships, and other life-altering circumstances. These modern-day miracles reinforce the belief that God's power to make a way is as active today as it was in biblical times.

In our own lives, we may encounter moments when the path ahead appears blocked by insurmountable obstacles. During these times, it is crucial to remember that God's perspective is far greater than our own. He sees the bigger picture and has a plan that transcends our understanding. Trusting in God's wisdom and timing can transform our approach to challenges, allowing us to move forward with faith and confidence.

Prayer plays a vital role in experiencing God's provision in impossible situations. When we bring our concerns and fears before God, we invite His presence into our circumstances. Through prayer, we acknowledge our dependence on Him and open ourselves to His guidance and intervention. Just as King Hezekiah laid out his desperate situation before God and received deliverance from the Assyrian threat, we too can find solace and direction through prayer.

Faith is another essential component in witnessing God's miraculous provision. In Hebrews faith is defines as "the assurance of things hoped for, the conviction of things not seen." This assurance and conviction enable us to trust in God's promises, even when our circumstances suggest otherwise. Faith empowers us to believe that God can and will make a way, despite the apparent impossibility of our situation.

In conclusion, the belief that God makes a way through what seems impossible at first is a powerful source of hope and strength. Biblical accounts and personal testimonies alike attest to God's ability to intervene in miraculous ways, transforming hopeless situations into triumphs. As we face our own challenges, we can draw inspiration from these stories, placing our trust in God's providence and experiencing His guidance and provision in our lives. Through prayer, faith, and a steadfast belief in God's power, we can navigate life's obstacles with confidence, knowing that with God, nothing is impossible.

"His followers would be victorious despite great obstacles by trusting in the Lord and obeying His Word, so be strong and courageous always."
