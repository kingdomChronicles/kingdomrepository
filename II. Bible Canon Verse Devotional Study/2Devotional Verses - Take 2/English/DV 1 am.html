Daily Devotional Verses

The Daily Devotional Program is designed to train us to grow in Christ through God's Word.

Instruction on how to successfully kickstart your Daily Devotional Lifestyle with the Lord.

  • Start your Daily Verse Devotion by praying earnestly to God that He will give you understanding and guidance in your devotion.
  • If you have your own Bible, open the verse in your bible else, read the verse from this page.
  • Next, read the Devotional Verse 3 times with an audible voice or louder. Let the enemy hear God's Word that is uttered in your lips.
  • Next, read the reflection portion, one or more until the thought of the verse reflection is taken into your heart.
  • Then, end your devotion with prayer having a thankful heart.
  • PAALALA: Ang matagumpay na Morning Devotion ay magiging gabay at proteksyon mo sa buong araw habang, ang matagumpay na Evening Devotion ay magiging gabay at proteksyon naman sa buong magdamag.

Bible Verse of the Day

2 Timothy 3:16-17

NIV (New International Version)

16All Scripture is God-breathed and is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting and training in righteousness,17 so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work.


GUIDE REFLECTION:


All Scripture is God's Breathed... All scriptures come from God, meaning inspired by the Holy Spirit. Some were saying that it is only written by man, yes it is written by man who is chosen by God with the inspiration of the Holy Spirit. Because the Scripture were written as instruction and a guide for mankind.

useful for teaching... The Scripture is beneficial to those who read it, and it is God's means to teach and guide all His called and chosen ones.

useful for rebuking and correcting... People have many customs, traditions, and practices that we do not know or are not aware of, and these are already acts of sin against God, we can use the scriptures to correct the wrong actions we do in our everyday walk in life.

    Sample of practices that God forbids

  • You shall not kill (Exodus 20:13)
  • You shall not steal (Exodus 20:15)
  • Gossip is wrong (Proverbs 20:19)
  • Instruction on excessive eating (Proverbs 23:20-21)
  • Lesson about greed (Luke 12:15)

useful in training in righteousness, Thus, God invites us to always listen, read, and meditate on His words so that we can be led to a sensible life and that we are not harmed in our daily lives and even after death.

so that the servant of God may be thoroughly equipped for every good work. This is a call to all servants of God or anyone who wants to be servants of God, we are invited to continuously be anchored in His Word so that we can always be ready at all times to do good things in our daily walks of life.
