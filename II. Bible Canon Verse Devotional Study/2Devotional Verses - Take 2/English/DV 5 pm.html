Daily Devotional Verses

The Daily Devotional Program is designed to train us to grow in Christ through God's Word.

Instruction on how to successfully kickstart your Daily Devotional Lifestyle with the Lord.

  • Start your Daily Verse Devotion by praying earnestly to God that He will give you understanding and guidance in your devotion.
  • If you have your own Bible, open the verse in your bible else, read the verse from this page.
  • Next, read the Devotional Verse 3 times with an audible voice or louder. Let the enemy hear God's Word that is uttered in your lips.
  • Next, read the reflection portion, one or more until the thought of the verse reflection is taken into your heart.
  • Then, end your devotion with prayer having a thankful heart.
  • Successful Morning Devotion will serve as your guidance and protection for the whole day while, Successful Evening Devotion will serve as your guidance and protection for the whole night.
  • Reflections submition: AM until 12:00 Noon, PM until 12 midnight.

Bible Verse of the Day - Evening

Romans 7:14

AMP (Amplified Version)

14 We know that the Law is spiritual, but I am a creature of the flesh [worldly, self-reliant-carnal and unspiritual], sold into slavery to sin [and serving under its control].


GUIDE REFLECTION:


A gladiator once remarked, "I fought valiantly in the arena and emerged victorious in every battle. However, the greatest battle I've faced is the one within myself, and in that battle, I often find myself defeated."

Apostle Paul clearly explains why most people repeatedly lose the same battle: "We know that the Law is spiritual, but I am a creature of the flesh-worldly, self-reliant, carnal, and unspiritual-sold into slavery to sin and serving under its control."

But I am carnal, sold into the slavery of sin. "Carnal" denotes a weakness toward sin due to our sin nature. The flesh resides within me, attacking relentlessly, and I am no match for it. On my own, and if left to my own means, I become its unwilling, resistant slave.

This is a conflict we cannot avoid, as we remain sinful unless God's power is at work within us. This is a common experience, even for those who are virtuous and attempt to achieve the righteousness of the Law through their own efforts. Sin is not only about committing wrong actions but also about trying to do what is right through our own strength.


When Paul considered God's holy Law and attempted to obey it, he recognized his sinful nature and his lack of power to keep the Law on his own. He realized that even the most virtuous person cannot overcome the flesh (sin nature) by self-effort alone. He had to come to terms with the presence of indwelling sin and his need for divine assistance to suppress it.

This is why it was mentioned in our earlier studies that even our good deeds are like filthy rags before the Lord. Good deeds we do are not considered as such if we are not within God's plan. We are invited to submit to His will and follow His words so that all our actions align with His will and acceptable in His sight. Absolutely, continuing to learn and understand God's Words can bring guidance and blessings to our lives.
