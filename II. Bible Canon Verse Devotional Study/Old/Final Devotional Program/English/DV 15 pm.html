Bible Verse of the Day - Evening

James 2:10

ESV (English Standard Version)

10 For whoever keeps the whole law but fails in one point has become guilty of all of it.


GUIDE REFLECTION:


In the previous verse, James insisted that showing favoritism to a wealthy person over a poor one is a sin. It means failing to obey God's command to love others as we love ourselves, making us lawbreakers.

James reveals that even breaking one command in the Law makes us guilty as if we had broken all of them. The point isn't that all sins are equally bad, but in God's eyes, a sin is still a sin, and we are guilty of breaking God's Royal Law.

Humans have only two choices: either a person is perfect and can be saved, or they are not, which is the reality for everyone. If someone "stumbles," they are considered to have "failed" just as much as if they had deliberately disobeyed God's law their whole life.

Even if we can't keep just one of the Ten Commandments, we are still guilty, and every violation has consequences. This means humans are in a lost state because we can never meet God's standard of Holiness.

That's why we need a perfect, sinless sacrifice to wash away our sins, something we cannot do on our own. We need God's rescue.

This is why we are encouraged to study God's Word to learn about the solution He provided. God made a way to help people because it's impossible for humans to find the path to salvation on their own.
