Daily Devotional Verses

The Daily Devotional Program for kids is designed to teach kids from 13 years old and below the truths written in God's Word.

Instruction:

  1. In the morning, simply read and understand the message of the passage for that day.
  2. Begin your Daily Devotion by praying sincerely to God for understanding and guidance throughout your day.
  3. If you have your own Bible, open the passage there; if not, read the passage from this page.
  4. Then, read the Devotional Passage out loud three times. Let the enemy hear God's Word coming from your lips.
  5. For your morning devotion, just meditate on the passage without including the guide reflection yet.
  6. Watch the video to better understand the passages we will study throughout the entire series, even just once for the whole series of your devotion.
  7. In the evening, read the reflection portion one or more times until you grasp and internalize the message of the passage.
  8. Answer the given question and submit your answer as your reflection.


Genesis Chapter 1-3 Series Lesson inspired by Superbook Story Series



Genesis 1:1

NIV (New International Version)

1"In the beginning, God created the heaven and the earth."


GUIDE REFLECTION:


"In the beginning, God created the heaven and the earth" means that before anything existed, God made everything from scratch! Think of it like starting with a blank sheet of paper and drawing something amazing. God created the heaven, which includes the sky and stars, and the earth, where we live with all the plants, animals, and people. This verse shows us that God is powerful and everything we see came from His imagination and love. It’s also a reminder that since God made the world, we can trust that everything has a purpose, including us!

What This Means:

1. A New Beginning: Imagine a story that starts from scratch. This verse tells us that there was a time when nothing existed—no trees, no animals, no people—just God. It’s like starting a blank page in a coloring book before you add your own colors!

2. Who is God?: God is like the ultimate creator! He is super powerful and can make anything He wants. He’s kind of like an artist who can create beautiful pictures, but instead, He created the whole world!

3. Creating Everything: When it says "Heaven and earth," it means God made everything we see around us. "Heaven" is where the stars and the sky are, and "earth" is our planet, where we live, play, and explore. God made the mountains, the oceans, the animals, and even you!

4. Why It’s Important: This verse is important because it tells us that everything has a special beginning and that God is in charge of it all. It reminds us that we are part of a big, amazing story created by God.

Questions:

1. What do you think the world looked like before God created the heavens and the earth? Describe what comes to your imagination.

2. Why do you think God created everything out of love and that we all have a purpose? How do you feel about yourself knowing that you are valued and loved by God?

3. When you look at the sky or see nature, how do your feelings about these things change knowing that God created everything?
