Proverbs 3:35

NASB (New American Standard Bible)

35 "The wise will inherit honor, But fools increase dishonor."


GUIDE REFLECTION:


Wisdom and Foolishness in the Light of Eternity

"The wise will inherit honor, but fools increase dishonor". This verse captures a profound truth about the consequences of wisdom and foolishness, not only in earthly life but also in eternity. It draws a clear line between those who live according to godly knowledge and those who reject it, emphasizing that wisdom leads to honor, while foolishness results in shame and disgrace.

In the biblical context, wisdom is not merely intellectual capability or the accumulation of knowledge. It is the ability to act in accordance with godly principles, to fear the Lord, and to walk in His ways. On the other hand, foolishness is not ignorance or lack of intelligence. Rather, it refers to a willful rejection of spiritual truth, stubbornness, arrogance, and the refusal to acknowledge God’s sovereignty. Fools mock godly wisdom, closing their hearts to the eternal perspective that God offers.

Throughout Scripture, the consequences of wisdom and foolishness are evident. Proverbs 3:31 warns against envy of the wicked, and in the Psalm of Asaph,

Philippians 3:18-19 NKJV
18 For many walk, of whom I have told you often, and now tell you even weeping, that they are the enemies of the cross of Christ: 19 whose end is destruction, whose god is their belly, and whose glory is in their shame—who set their mind on earthly things.

This identifies such people as "enemies of the cross of Christ," whose destiny is destruction. A striking example of this is the parable of the rich fool in Luke.

Luke 12:16-21 NLT
16 Then he told them a story: “A rich man had a fertile farm that produced fine crops. 17 He said to himself, ‘What should I do? I don’t have room for all my crops.’ 18 Then he said, ‘I know! I’ll tear down my barns and build bigger ones. Then I’ll have room enough to store all my wheat and other goods. 19 And I’ll sit back and say to myself, “My friend, you have enough stored away for years to come. Now take it easy! Eat, drink, and be merry!”’ 20 “But God said to him, ‘You fool! You will die this very night. Then who will get everything you worked for?’ 21 “Yes, a person is a fool to store up earthly wealth but not have a rich relationship with God.”

The man trusted in his wealth and boasted in his material abundance, but God declared him a fool for failing to consider the eternal state of his soul. That very night, his life was taken, and all his possessions became meaningless.

In contrast, the wise inherit honor by trusting in God and living according to His will. Honor may not always be fully realized in this life, as believers often face trials and opposition, but it is guaranteed in eternity. The apostle Paul serves as a prime example of wisdom in action. Despite suffering for the sake of the gospel, Paul remained faithful and looked forward to the crown of righteousness that the Lord would award him.

2 Timothy 4:8 NLT
And now the prize awaits me—the crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous Judge, will give me on the day of his return. And the prize is not just for me but for all who eagerly look forward to his appearing.

He assured that this reward was not limited to him but extended to all who longed for Christ’s return.

Ultimately, Proverbs 3:35 reminds us of the stark difference between wisdom and foolishness. Fools pursue fleeting pleasures, trusting in worldly success or material wealth, only to find themselves empty-handed before God. The wise, however, invest in what is eternal—trusting in God, living for His glory, and walking in His ways. They may lose much in this world, but they gain eternal honor and reward.

In the end, wisdom and foolishness are choices that define our eternal destiny. To walk in wisdom is to align ourselves with God’s truth and to embrace the life He offers. To walk in foolishness is to reject His truth, leading to dishonor and destruction. Let us choose the path of wisdom, so that we may inherit honor both now and in eternity.
