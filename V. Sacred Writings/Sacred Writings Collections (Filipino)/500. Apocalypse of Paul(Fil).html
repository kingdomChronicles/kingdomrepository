May kilala akong isang Cristiano na dinala sa ikatlong langit, labing-apat na taon na ang nakakalipas. Hindi ko lang matiyak kung iyo'y isang pangitain lamang, o tunay na pangyayari; ang Diyos lamang ang nakakaalam. Inuulit ko, siya'y dinala sa Paraiso. At tulad ng sabi ko, ang Diyos lamang ang nakakaalam kung ito nga'y isang pangitain o tunay na pangyayari. Nakarinig siya roon ng mga bagay na di kayang ilarawan ng salita at di dapat bigkasin ninuman. Ipagmamalaki ko ang taong iyon, at hindi ang aking sarili, maliban sa aking mga kahinaan.”— 2 Mga Taga-Corinto 12:1-5



Dito Nagsisimula ang Pangitain ni San Pablo na isang Apostol.


Chapter 1

Ang Pangitain

1 Sa anong panahon ipinahayag ang pahayag na ito?
2 Sa panahon ng pagka-konsul ni Theodosius Augustus na Bata at ni Cynegius, isang marangal na lalaki na naninirahan noon sa Tharsus,
3 sa bahay ni San Pablo, ay nagpakita sa kanya ang isang anghel sa gabi,
4 sinasabing buksan niya ang mga pundasyon ng bahay at ipahayag ang kanyang matutuklasan.
5 Ngunit inisip niya na ito’y panaginip lamang.

Chapter 2

Natagpuan ang Pahayag ni San Pablo

1 Ngunit sa ikatlong pagdating ng anghel, pinalo siya nito at pinilit siyang buksan ang pundasyon.
2 Sa paghuhukay, nakakita siya ng isang kahon na marmol na may nakaukit sa mga gilid;
3 naroon ang pahayag ni San Pablo, at ang kanyang mga sandalyas na kanyang ginamit sa pagtuturo ng salita ng Diyos.
4 Ngunit natakot siyang buksan ang kahon at dinala ito sa hukom;
5 nang matanggap ito ng hukom, dahil ito’y nakaselyo ng tingga, ipinadala niya ito kay Emperador Theodosius,
6 sa takot na baka may ibang laman ito; at nang matanggap ito ng emperador, binuksan niya ito at natagpuan ang pahayag ni San Pablo;
7 Isang kopya nito ang ipinadala niya sa Jerusalem, at itinago niya ang orihinal sa kanyang sarili.


Chapter 3

Ang tao ang Tanging Nilikha na Lumalapastangan sa Lumikha

1 Habang ako ay nasa katawan kung saan ako'y dinala sa ikatlong langit,
2 ang salita ng Panginoon ay dumating sa akin,
3 sinasabing: ipahayag ito sa mga tao:
4 hanggang kailan kayo magpapatuloy sa pagsuway, magdagdag sa patong patong na kasalanan sa kasalanan, at susubukin ang Panginoon na lumikha sa inyo?
5 Kayo’y mga anak ng Diyos sa pananampalataya kay Cristo, subalit ginagawa ang mga gawa ng diyablo, dahil sa mga bagay na makasanlibutan.
6 Kaya’t alalahanin at unawain na habang ang lahat ng nilikha ay naglilingkod sa Diyos,
7 ang lahi ng tao lamang ang niliklhang nagkakasala.
8 Habang ito’y naghahari sa lahat ng nilikha at nagkakasala nang higit sa lahat ng kalikasan.


Chapter 4

Ang Panawagan ng Araw sa Diyos

1 Tunay ngang ang araw, ang dakilang liwanag, ay madalas na nananawagan sa Panginoon na nagsasabing:
2 Panginoon Diyos na Makapangyarihan, nakikita ko ang mga kawalang-paggalang at kawalang-katarungan ng mga tao;
3 pahintulutan mo akong gawin sa kanila ang aking mga kakayahan, upang malaman nila na ikaw lamang ang Diyos.
4 At dumating ang isang tinig na nagsabi sa kanya:
5 Alam ko ang lahat ng mga bagay na ito, sapagkat ang aking mata ay nakakakita at ang aking tainga ay nakakarinig,
6 ngunit ang aking pagtitiis ay nagpapahintulot sa kanila hanggang sila'y magbalik-loob at magsisi.
7 Ngunit kung hindi sila babalik sa akin, huhusgahan ko silang lahat.


Chapter 5

Ang Panawagan ng Buwan at ng mga Bituin

1 Minsan ang buwan at mga bituin ay nananawagan din sa Panginoon na nagsasabing:
2 Panginoon Diyos na Makapangyarihan, binigyan mo kami ng kapangyarihan ng gabi;
3 hanggang kailan kami magmamasid sa kawalang-galang, pakikiapid, at pagpatay na ginagawa ng mga anak ng tao?
4 Pahintulutan mo kaming gawin sa kanila ayon sa aming kakayahan,
5 upang malaman nila na ikaw lamang ang Diyos.
6 At dumating ang isang tinig sa kanila na nagsasabing:
7 Alam ko ang lahat ng mga bagay na ito, at ang aking mata ay nakamasid at ang aking tainga ay nakaririnig,
8 ngunit ang aking pagtitiis ay nagpapahintulot sa kanila hanggang sila'y magbalik-loob at magsisi.
9 Ngunit kung hindi sila babalik sa akin, huhusgahan ko sila.


Chapter 6

Ang Pagtangis din naman ng Dagat

1 At madalas din na tumatangis ang dagat, at nagsasabing:
2 Panginoon Diyos na Makapangyarihan, nilalapastangan ng mga tao ang iyong banal na pangalan sa akin;
3 pahintulutan mo akong bumangon at takpan ang bawat kagubatan at taniman at ang buong mundo, hanggang sa maubos ko ang lahat ng anak ng tao mula sa iyong harapan,
4 upang malaman nila na ikaw lamang ang Diyos.
5 At muling dumating ang tinig at nagsabi:
6 Alam ko ang lahat ng bagay; nakikita ng aking mata ang lahat, at naririnig ng aking tainga,
7 ngunit ang aking pagtitiis ay nagpapahintulot sa kanila hanggang sila'y magbalik-loob at magsisi.
8 Ngunit kung hindi sila magbabalik, sila’y aking hahatulan.

Chapter 7

Nagreklamo din ang mga Tubig dahil sa Kasalanan ng mga Tao

1 Minsan ang tubig din ay nagreklamo laban sa mga kasalanan ng tao, na nagsasabi:
2 Panginoon Diyos na Makapangyarihan, nilapastangan ng lahat ng anak ng tao ang iyong banal na pangalan.
3 At dumating ang isang tinig na nagsabi:
4 Alam ko ang lahat ng bagay bago pa ito mangyari, sapagkat ang aking mata ay nakakakita at ang aking tainga ay nakakarinig ng lahat,
5 ngunit ang aking pagtitiis ay nagpapahintulot sa kanila hanggang sila’y magbalik-loob.
6 Ngunit kung hindi, sila’y aking hahatulan.

Chapter 8

Ang Madalas na Pagtangis ng Lupa sa Panginoon

1 Madalas din na tumatangis ang lupa sa Panginoon laban sa mga anak ng tao, na nagsasabing:
2 Panginoon Diyos na Makapangyarihan, ako sa lahat ng iyong nilikha ang labis na naaapektuhan,
3 na sumusuporta sa pakikiapid, pangangalunya, pagpatay, pagnanakaw, pagsisinungaling, mahika, at masasamang gawa ng tao, at lahat ng kasamaan na kanilang ginagawa,
4 hanggang sa maghimagsik ang ama laban sa anak,
5 at ang anak laban sa ama,
6 ang banyaga laban sa banyaga,
7 kaya’t bawat isa ay nilalapastangan ang asawa ng kanyang kapwa.
8 Ang ama ay aakyat sa higaan ng kanyang sariling anak,
8 at ang anak ay gayon din aakyat sa kama ng kanyang sariling ama;
8 at sa lahat ng mga kasamaang ito, sila na nag-aalay ng sakripisyo sa iyong pangalan ay nilapastangan ang iyong banal na lugar.
9 Kaya’t ako ay labis na naapektuhan kaysa sa lahat ng nilikha, nagnanais na hindi ipakita ang aking kapangyarihan at ang aking bunga sa mga anak ng tao.
10 Pahintulutan mo ako at wawasakin ko ang bisa ng aking mga bunga.
11 At dumating ang tinig at nagsabi:
12 Alam ko ang lahat ng bagay, at walang makakapagtago ng kanyang kasalanan sa akin.
13 Bukod dito, alam ko ang kanilang kawalang-galang, ngunit ang aking kabanalan ay nagtitiis hanggang sila’y magbalik-loob at magsisi.
14 Ngunit kung hindi sila magbabalik sa akin, sila’y aking hahatulan.


Chapter 9

Ang Araw-Araw na Pagtatala sa lahat ng Gawain ng Tao at ng Bansa sa Pamamagitan ng Kanilang mga Bantay na Angel

1 Narito, kayong mga anak ng tao, ang lahat ng nilikha ay sumusunod sa Diyos,
1 ngunit ang lahi ng tao lamang ang nagkakasala.
2 Kaya’t kayong mga anak ng tao, purihin ang Panginoon Diyos nang walang patid, bawat oras at bawat araw:
3 higit lalo kapag lumubog na ang araw,
4 sapagkat sa oras na iyon ang lahat ng mga anghel ay nagpupunta sa Panginoon upang sambahin siya at iharap ang mga gawa ng tao,
5 mabuti man o masama, na ginawa ng bawat tao mula sa umaga hanggang sa gabi.
6 At may isang anghel na masayang nagpupunta ukol sa taong kanyang pinaninirahan.
7 Kaya’t kapag lumubog ang araw sa unang oras ng gabi,
8 sa oras ding iyon ang anghel ng bawat bayan, lalaki, at babae,
9 na nagpoprotekta at nag-iingat sa kanila, sapagkat ang tao ay larawan ng Diyos:
10 gayon din sa umagang oras na ikalabindalawa ng gabi,
11 lahat ng anghel ng mga tao lalaki o babae man ay umaakyat sa Diyos upang sambahin siya at iharap ang bawat gawa ng tao, mabuti man o masama.
12 Bukod pa dito, bawat araw at gabi, ipinapakita ng mga anghel sa Diyos ang ulat ng lahat ng mga gawa ng sangkatauhan.
13 Kaya’t sinasabi ko sa inyo, mga anak ng tao,
14 purihin ang Panginoon Diyos nang walang patid sa lahat ng araw ng inyong buhay.


Chapter 10

Ang Pagsamba at Pagpupulong ng mga Anghel sa Presensiya ng Diyos

1 Kaya’t sa itinakdang oras, lahat ng anghel, sa kanilang sabay-sabay na kasiyahan, ay nagpupunta sa harap ng Diyos upang magtipon at sumamba sa itinakdang oras.
2 At narito, biglang dumating ang oras ng pagtitipon, at ang mga anghel ay dumating upang sumamba sa presensya ng Diyos, at ang espiritu ay nagpunta upang salubungin sila:
3 at dumating ang isang tinig at nagsabi: Saan kayo nagmula, mga anghel namin, dala ang mga balita?


Chapter 11

Ang Presensiya ng Diyos at nang mga Anghel sa mga Taong Nagtitiis at Kinalimutan ang Mundo para sa Ikaluluwalhati ng Diyos

1 Sumagot sila at nagsabi: Kami ay nagmula sa mga tumalikod sa mundong ito alang-alang sa iyong banal na pangalan, naglalagalag bilang mga peregrino, sa mga kuweba ng mga bato, at umiiyak bawat oras habang sila’y nabubuhay sa lupa, gutom at nauuhaw alang-alang sa iyong pangalan, na nakagapos ang kanilang baywang, dala ang insenso ng kanilang puso, nananalangin at nagpupuri sa bawat oras, pinipigil at nilalabanan ang kanilang sarili, umiiyak at nananaghoy ng higit sa iba pang naninirahan sa lupa.
2 Kami, mga anghel nila, ay tumatangis kasama nila: kaya’t saan mo man nais, utusan mo kaming maglingkod sa kanila, upang hindi sila pabayaan, lalo na ang mga nangangailangan kaysa sa iba sa lupa.
3 At dumating ang tinig ng Diyos sa kanila, na nagsasabing: Alamin ninyo na simula ngayon ay ipinagkaloob ko sa inyo ang aking biyaya, at ang aking tulong, ang aking minamahal na Anak, ay sasamahan sila, ginagabayan sila bawat oras; maglilingkod din sa kanila, hindi sila iiwanan, sapagkat ang kanilang lugar ay ang kanyang tahanan.


Chapter 12

Ang mga Anghel na Dumalo ng Pagtitipon upang Sumamba na dala ang kanilang Pag-iyak.

1 Nang umalis ang mga anghel na ito, narito ang ibang mga anghel ay dumating upang sumamba sa presensya ng karangalan, sa pagtitipon, na umiiyak;
2 at ang espiritu ng Diyos ay nagpunta upang salubungin sila, at dumating ang tinig ng Diyos at nagsabi:
3 Saan kayo nagmula, mga anghel namin, dala ang mga balita ng paglilingkod sa mundo?
4 Sila’y sumagot at nagsabi sa presensya ng Diyos:
5 Dumating kami mula sa mga tumatawag sa iyong pangalan,
6 ngunit ang mga hadlang ng mundo ay nagpapahirap sa kanila,
7 na nagdudulot ng maraming pagkakataon sa bawat oras,
8 ni hindi makapagdasal ng taimtim at dalisay,
9 ni magmula sa kaibuturan ng kanilang puso,
10 sa lahat ng pagkakataon ng kanilang buhay;
11 ano nga ba ang dahilan upang manatili kami sa piling ng mga makasalanang tao?
12 At dumating ang tinig ng Diyos sa kanila:
13 Kinakailangang maglingkod kayo sa kanila, hanggang sila’y magbalik-loob at magsisi:
14 ngunit kung hindi sila magbabalik sa akin, sila’y aking hahatulan.
15 Kaya’t alamin ninyo, mga anak ng tao, na anuman ang inyong mga ginagawa,
16 ang mga anghel na ito ay ipinapahayag ang mga ito sa Diyos, mabuti man o masama.


Chapter 13

Ang mga Anghel na Nakatalaga sa mga Hindi Maka-Diyos

1 At sinagot ako ng anghel at sinabi:
2 Sundan mo ako, at ipapakita ko sa iyo ang lugar ng mga matuwid kung saan sila dinadala kapag sila'y yumao,
3 at pagkatapos ng mga ito, dadalhin kita sa kailaliman,
4 at ipapakita ko sa iyo ang mga kaluluwa ng mga makasalanan at ang uri ng lugar na kanilang patutunguhan kapag sila'y namatay.
5 Kaya sumunod ako sa anghel,
6 at dinala niya ako sa langit, at lumingon ako sa kalawakan,
7 at nakita ko doon ang kapangyarihan,
8 at naroroon ang pagkakalimot na nanlilinlang at umaakit sa puso ng mga tao,
9 at ang espiritu ng paninirang-puri,
10 at ang espiritu ng kahalayan,
11 at ang espiritu ng kabaliwan,
12 at ang espiritu ng pagmamataas,
13 at naroroon ang mga pinuno ng mga bisyo;
14 nakita ko silang lahat sa ilalim ng kalangitan: at muli akong lumingon,
15 at nakita ko ang mga anghel na walang habag, na walang awa,
16 ang kanilang mga mukha'y puno ng galit,
17 at ang kanilang mga ngipin ay labis na nakausli sa kanilang mga bibig;
18 ang kanilang mga mata ay nagniningning na parang tala ng umaga sa silangan,
19 at mula sa kanilang buhok ay lumalabas ang mga apoy, maging sa kanilang mga bibig.
20 At tinanong ko ang anghel, sinasabing: Sino sila?
21 At sinagot ako ng anghel at sinabi:
22 Sila ang mga nakatalaga sa mga kaluluwa ng mga di-makadiyos sa oras ng kanilang paglisan,
23 ang mga hindi naniwala na ang Panginoon ang kanilang tagapagligtas, ni umasa sa Kanya.


Chapter 14

Ang mga Anghel na Nakatalaga sa mga Maka-Diyos

1 At tumingin ako sa itaas at nakita ang ibang mga anghel na ang mukha ay nagniningning na parang araw,
2 ang kanilang mga baywang ay may gintong pamigkis,
3 may hawak na mga palaspas sa kanilang mga kamay,
4 at may tanda ng Diyos,
5 nakasuot ng damit na may nakasulat na pangalan ng Anak ng Diyos,
6 na puspos ng kababaang-loob at awa;
7 at tinanong ko ang mga anghel:
8 Sino sila, Panginoon, na napakaganda at maawain?
9 At sinagot ako ng anghel at sinabi:
10 Sila ang mga anghel ng katarungan na ipinadala upang ihatid ang mga kaluluwa ng mga matuwid sa oras ng kanilang paglisan,
11 ang mga naniwala na ang Panginoon ang kanilang tagapagligtas.
12 At sinabi ko sa kanya: Ang mga matuwid ba at makasalanan ay kinakailangang humarap sa mga saksi kapag sila ay namatay?
13 At sinagot ako ng anghel at sinabi:
14 May iisang daan na dinadaanan ng lahat patungo sa Diyos,
15 ngunit ang mga matuwid na kasama ang kanilang tagapag-alalay ay hindi nagugulumihanan kapag sila'y humarap sa Diyos.


Chapter 15

Ang Presensiya ng Apoy na Sumisimbolo sa Kasamaan

1 At sinabi ko sa anghel: Nais kong makita ang mga kaluluwa ng mga matuwid at ng mga makasalanan sa kanilang paglabas mula sa mundo.
2 At sinagot ako ng anghel at sinabi: Tumingin ka sa lupa.
3 At tumingin ako mula sa langit patungo sa lupa,
4 at nakita ko ang buong mundo, at tila wala naman akong nakita na kakaiba sa aking nakita
5 at nakita ko ang mga anak ng tao na parang wala,
6 at nagtataka ako at sinabi sa anghel: Ito ba ang kadakilaan ng tao?
7 At sinagot ako ng anghel at sinabi:
8 Oo, at sila ang mga gumagawa ng masama mula umaga hanggang gabi.
9 At tumingin ako at nakita ang malaking ulap ng apoy na kumalat sa buong mundo,
10 at sinabi ko sa anghel: Ano ito, Panginoon ko?
11 At sinabi niya sa akin: Ito ay kawalang-katarungang inudyok ng mga pinuno ng mga makasalanan.


Sa mga nabuksan ang kanilang espiritual na mata, mararamdaman mo ang matinding espirituwal na kadiliman, na sinisimbolo ng ulap ng apoy, sa mga lugar kung saan laganap ang kasamaan o kawalang galang sa Diyos. Kasama rito ang mga sugalan, lugar ng imoralidad, at mga simbahan o templo na puno ng idolatrya at maling pagsamba.


Chapter 16

The Manner a Dying Just Man Go Out in the Body

1 Nang marinig ko ito, ako ay napabuntong-hininga at umiyak,
2 at sinabi ko sa anghel: Nais kong makita ang mga kaluluwa ng mga matuwid at ng mga makasalanan,
3 at makita kung paano sila umaalis sa kanilang mga katawan.
4 At sinagot ako ng anghel at sinabi: Tumingin ka muli sa lupa.
5 At tumingin ako at nakita ko ang buong mundo, at ang mga tao ay tila walang halaga at kulang:
6 at tinitigan ko at nakita ko ang isang lalaking malapit nang mamatay, at sinabi ng anghel sa akin:
7 Ang taong ito na nakikita mo ay isang matuwid na tao.
8 At muli akong tumingin at nakita ko ang lahat ng kanyang mga gawa, lahat ng kanyang ginawa alang-alang sa pangalan ng Diyos, at lahat ng kanyang mga pagnanais, kapwa ang kanyang naalala, at ang hindi niya naalala;
9 silang lahat ay nasa harapan niya sa oras ng paglisan;
10 at nakita ko ang matuwid na tao na nakatagpo ng kaluwagan at kapanatagan,
at bago siya lumisan mula sa mundo, ang banal at mga anghel ng kasamaan ay parehong naroroon:
11 at nakita ko silang lahat, ngunit walang lugar na nahanap ang mga anghel ng kasamaan sa kanya,
12 subalit ang mga anghel na nakatalaga sa mga banal ay nag-angkin ng kanyang kaluluwa, pinapatnubayan ito hanggang sa ito ay umalis sa katawan:
13 at ginising nila ang kaluluwa na sinasabi: Kaluluwa, alamin ang iyong katawan kung saan ka lumisan,
14 sapagkat kinakailangang bumalik ka sa parehong katawan sa araw ng muling pagkabuhay,
15 upang matanggap mo ang mga pangakong inilaan para sa lahat ng matuwid.
16Kaya't tinanggap nila ang kaluluwa mula sa katawan, hinalikan ito nang may pagpapahalaga na parang kaisa na nila, sinasabing:
17 Magpakatatag ka, sapagkat ginawa mo ang kalooban ng Diyos habang ikaw ay nasa lupa.
18 At sumalubong sa kanya ang anghel na nagbabantay sa kanya araw-araw, at sinabi sa kanya:
19 Magpakatatag ka, kaluluwa; sapagkat natutuwa ako sa iyo, sapagkat ginawa mo ang kalooban ng Diyos sa lupa:
20 sapagkat isinalaysay ko sa Diyos ang lahat ng iyong mga gawa, ayon sa pagkakagawa ng mga ito.
21 Gayundin, lumapit ang espiritu upang salubungin siya at sinabi:
22 Kaluluwa, huwag kang matakot, ni magulumihanan, hanggang makarating ka sa isang lugar na hindi mo pa nakikilala,
23 ngunit ako'y magiging tagapagligtas mo: sapagkat natagpuan ko sa iyo ang isang lugar ng kaginhawahan noong ako ay nanahan sa iyo, habang ako ay nasa lupa.
24 At pinalakas siya ng kanyang espiritu, at tinanggap siya ng kanyang anghel, at dinala siya sa langit:
25 at sinabi ng isang anghel: Saan ka tumatakbo, O kaluluwa, at ikaw ba'y nangangahas na pumasok sa langit?
26 Maghintay at tingnan natin kung may anuman sa iyo na sa amin;
27 at narito wala kaming nakita sa iyo.
28 Nakikita ko rin ang iyong banal na tagapag-alalay at anghel, at ang espiritu ay natutuwa sa iyo, sapagkat ginawa mo ang kalooban ng Diyos sa lupa.
29 At pinatnubayan nila siya hanggang siya ay sumamba sa harapan ng Diyos.
30 At nang sila'y huminto, agad na si Miguel at ang lahat ng hukbo ng mga anghel,
31 sa iisang tinig, ay sumamba sa harapan ng kanyang mga paa, at sinasabi sa kaluluwa:
32 Ito ang iyong Diyos sa lahat ng bagay, na lumikha sa iyo ayon sa kanyang sariling imahe at wangis.
33 At ang anghel ay bumalik at ipinakita siya na sinasabi:
34 Aming Diyos, alalahanin ang kanyang mga gawain: sapagkat ito ang kaluluwa, na ang mga gawa ay aking isinalaysay sa iyo, na gumagawa ayon sa iyong hatol.
35 At gayundin ang espiritu ay nagsabi:
36 Ako ang espiritu ng pagbibigay-buhay na nagbibigay inspirasyon sa kanya; sapagkat nakatagpo ako ng kaginhawahan sa kanya noong nanahan ako sa kanya, ginagawa niya ang naaayon sa iyong kalooban.
37 At narinig ang tinig ng Diyos na nagsasabi: Sapagkat ang taong ito ay hindi ako sinaktan, ni hindi ko rin siya sasaktan; sapagkat kung paanong siya ay nagpakita ng awa, ako rin ay magpapakita ng awa.
38 Kaya't ipagkaloob siya kay Miguel, ang anghel ng Tipan,
39 at hayaan siyang dalhin siya sa Paraiso ng kagalakan,
40 upang siya mismo ay maging kapwa tagapagmana ng lahat ng mga banal.
41 Pagkatapos ng mga bagay na ito, narinig ko ang tinig ng libu-libong anghel, mga arkanghel, mga kerubin, at dalawampu't apat na matatanda na umaawit ng mga himno,
42 pinararangalan ang Panginoon at sumisigaw: "Matuwid Ka, O Panginoon, at matuwid ang iyong mga hatol;
43 Wala Kang itinatangi, kundi gantimpalaan Mo ang bawat tao ayon sa iyong katarungan."
44 At sinabi sa akin ng anghel: "Naniniwala ka ba at nalalaman na ang anumang nagawa ng bawat isa sa inyo ay kanyang makikita sa oras ng paglisan?"
45 At ako'y sumagot, "Opo, ginoo."


Chapter 15

1And he saith to me: Look again down on the earth, and watch the soul of an impious man going out of the body, which vexed the Lord day and night, saying:
2I know nothing else in this world, I eat and drink, and enjoy what is in the world; for who is there who has descended into hell, and ascending has declared to us that there is judgment there!
3And again I looked carefully, and saw all the scorn of the sinner, and all that he did, and they stood together before him in the hour of need:
4and it was done to him in that hour, in which he was threatened about his body at the judgment, and I said:
5It were better for him if he had not been born.
6And after these things, there came at the same time, the holy angels, and the malign, and the soul of the sinner and the holy angels did not find a place in it.
7Moreover the malign angels cursed it; and when they had drawn it out of the body, the angels admonished it a third time, saying:
8O wretched soul, look upon thy flesh, whence thou camest out: for it is necessary that thou shouldst return to thy flesh in the day of resurrection, that thou mayest receive the due for thy sins and thy impieties.


Chapter 16

1And when they had led it forth, the customary angel preceded it, and said to it:
2O wretched soul, I am the angel belonging to thee, relating daily to the Lord thy malign works, whatever thou didst by night or day: and if it were in my power, not for one day would I minister to thee, but none of these things was I able to do:
3the judge is pitiful and just, and he himself commanded us that we should not cease to minister to the soul, till you should repent, but thou hast lost the time of repentance.
4I indeed was strange to thee and thou to me.
5Let us go on then to the just judge: I will not dismiss thee, before I know from to-day why I was strange to thee.
6And the spirit confounded him, and the angel troubled him.
7When, therefore, they had arrived at the power, when he started to enter heaven, a labour was imposed upon him, above all other labour:
8error and oblivion and murmuring met him, and the spirit of fornication, and the rest of the powers, and said to him:
9Whither goest thou, wretched soul, and darest thou to rush into heaven? hold, that we may see if we have our qualities in thee, since we do not see that thou hast a holy helper.
10And after that I heard voices in the height of heaven saying:
11Present that wretched soul to God, that it may know that it is God that it despised.
12When, therefore, it had entered heaven, all the angels saw it, a thousand thousand exclaimed with one voice, all saying:
13Woe to thee, wretched soul, for the sake of thy works which thou didst on earth; what answer art thou about to give to God when thou shalt have approached to adore him? The angel who was with it answered and said:
14Weep with me, my beloved, for I have not found rest in this soul.
15And the angels answered him and said:
16Let such a soul be taken away from the midst of ours, for from the time he entered, the stink of him crosses to us angels.
17And after these things it was presented, that it might worship in the sight of God, and an angel of God showed him God who made him after his own image and likeness.
18Moreover his angel ran before him saying:
19Lord God Almighty, I am the angel of this soul, whose works I presented to thee day and night, not doing according to thy judgment.
20And the spirit likewise said: I am the spirit who dwelt in it from the time it was made, in itself moreover I know it, and it has not followed my will: judge it, Lord, according to thy judgment.
21And there came the voice of God to it and said: Where is thy fruit which thou has made worthy of the goods which thou hast received?
22Have I put a distance of one day between thee and the just man?
23Did I not make the sun to arise upon thee as upon the just?
24But the soul was silent, having nothing to answer: and again there came a voice saying:
25Just is the judgment of God, and there is no acceptance of persons with God, for whoever shall have done mercy, on them shall he have mercy, and whoever shall not have pitied neither shall God pity him.
26Let him therefore be handed over to the angel Tartaruch, who is set over the punishments, and let him place him in outer darkness, where there is weeping and gnashing of teeth, and let him be there till the great day of judgment.
27And after these things I heard the voice of angels and archangels saying: Thou art just, Lord, and thy judgment is just.


Chapter 17

1And again I saw, and behold a soul which was led forward by two angels, weeping and saying:
2Have pity on me, just God, God the judge, for to-day is seven days since I went out of my body, and I was handed over to these two angels, and they led me through to those places, which I had never seen.
3And God, the just judge, saith to him: What hast thou done?
4for thou never didst mercy, wherefore thou wast handed over to such angels as have no mercy, and because thou didst not do uprightly, so neither did they act piously with thee in the hour of thy need.
5Confess therefore thy sins which thou didst commit when placed in the world.
6And he answered and said: Lord, I did not sin.
7And the Lord, the just Lord, was angered in fury when it said:
8I did not sin, because it lied; and God said:
9Dost thou think thou art still in the world? if any one of you, sinning there, conceal and hide his sin from his neighbour, here indeed nothing whatever shall be hid:
10for when the souls come to adore in sight of the throne, both the good works and the sins of each one are made manifest.
11And hearing these things the soul was silent, having no answer.
12And I heard the Lord God, the just judge, again saying: Come, angel of this soul, and stand in the midst.
13And the angel of the sinful soul came, having in his hands a manuscript, and said:
14These, Lord, in my hands, are all the sins of this soul from his youth till to-day, from the tenth year of his birth:
15and if thou command, Lord, I will also relate his acts from the beginning of his fifteenth year.
16And the Lord God, the just judge, said:
17I say unto thee, angel, I do not expect of thee an account of him since he began to be fifteen years old, but state his sins for five years before he died and before he came hither.
18And again God, the just judge, said:
19For by myself I swear, and by my holy angels, and by my virtue, that if he had repented five years before he died, on account of one year’s life, oblivion would now be thrown over all the evils which he sinned before, and he would have indulgence and remission of sins: now indeed he shall perish.
20And the angel of the sinful soul answered and said: Lord, command that angel to exhibit those souls.


Chapter 18

1And in that same hour the souls were exhibited in the midst, and the soul of the sinner knew them; and the Lord said to the soul of the sinner:
2I say unto thee, soul, confess thy work which thou wroughtest in these souls, whom thou seest, when they were in the world.
3And he answered and said: Lord, it is not yet a full year since I slew this one and poured his blood upon the ground, and with another (a woman) I committed fornication: not this alone, but I also greatly harmed her in taking away her goods.
4And the Lord God, the just judge, said: Either thou didst not know that he who does violence to another, if he dies first who sustains the violence, is kept in this place until the doer of hurt dies, and then both stand in the presence of the judge, and now each receives according to his deed.
5And I heard a voice of one saying: Let that soul be delivered into the hands of Tartarus, and led down into hell: he shall lead him into the lower prison and he shall be put in torments, and left there till the great day of judgment.
6And again I heard a thousand thousand angels saying hymns to the Lord, and crying:
7Thou art just, O Lord, and just are thy judgments.


Chapter 19

1The angel answered and said unto me: Hast thou perceived all these things? and I said, Yes, sir.
2And he said to me: Follow me again, and I will take thee, and show thee the places of the just.
3And I followed the angel, and he raised me to the third heaven, and placed me at the entry of the door: and looking carefully I saw, and the door was of gold, and two columns of gold, full above of golden letters, and the angel tuned again to me and said:
4Blessed wert thou, if thou hadst entered into these doors, for it is not allowed to any to enter except only to those who have goodness and innocence of body in all things.
5And I asked the angel about everything and said: Sir, tell me on what account these letters are put upon those tables?
6The angel answered and said unto me:
7These are the names of the just, serving God with their whole heart, who dwell on the earth.
8And again I said: Sir, therefore their names and countenance and the likeness of these who serve God are in heaven, and are known to the angels:
9for they know who are the servants of God with all their heart, before they go out of the world.


Chapter 20

1And when I had entered the interior of the gate of Paradise, there came out to meet me an old man whose countenance shone as the sun; and when he had embraced me he said:
2Hail, Paul, beloved of God. And he kissed me with a cheerful countenance.
3He wept, and I said to him: Brother, why dost thou weep?
4And again sighing and lamenting he said:
5We are hurt by men, and they vex us greatly; for many are the good things which the Lord has prepared, and great is his promise, but many do not perceive them.
6And I asked the angel, and said: Sir, who is this?
7And he said to me: This is Enoch, the scribe of righteousness.
8And I entered into the interior of that place, and immediately I saw the sun, and coming it saluted me laughing and rejoicing.
9And when it had seen (me), it turned away and wept, and said to me:
10Paul, would that thou shouldst receive thy labours which thou hast done in the human race.
11For me, indeed, I have seen the great and many good things, which God has prepared for the just, and the promises of God are great, but many do not perceive them;
12but even by many labours scarcely one or two enters into these places.


Chapter 21

1And the angel answered and said to me, Whatever I now show thee here, and whatever thou shalt hear, tell it not to any one in the earth.
2And he led me and shewed me: and there I heard words which it is not lawful for a man to speak.
3And again he said, For now follow me, and I will shew thee what thou oughtest to narrate in public and relate.
4And he took me down from the third heaven, and led me into the second heaven, and again he led me on to the firmament and from the firmament he led me over the doors of heaven:
5the beginning of its foundation was on the river which waters all the earth.
6And I asked the angel and said, Lord, what is this river of water?
7and he said to me, This is Oceanus!
8And suddenly I went out of heaven, and I understood that it is the light of heaven which lightens all the earth.
9For the land there is seven times brighter than silver.
10And I said, Lord, what is this place? And he said to me, This is the land of promise.
11Hast thou never heard what is written: Blessed are the meek:
12for they shall inherit the earth? The souls therefore of the just, when they have gone out of the body, are meanwhile dismissed to this place.
13And I said to the angel, Then this land will be manifested before the time?
14The angel answered and said to me, When Christ, whom thou preachest, shall come to reign, then, by the sentence of God, the first earth will be dissolved and this land of promise will then be revealed, and it will be like dew or cloud, and then the Lord Jesus Christ, the King Eternal, will be manifested and will come with all his saints to dwell in it, and he will reign over them a thousand years, and they will eat of the good things which I shall now show unto thee.


Chapter 22

1And I looked around upon that land and I saw a river flowing of milk and honey, and there were trees planted by the bank of that river, full of fruit:
2moreover each single tree bore twelve fruits in the year, having various and diverse fruits:
3and I saw the created things which are in that place and all the work of God, and I saw there palms of twenty cubits, but others of ten cubits:
4and that land was seven times brighter than silver.
5And there were trees full of fruits from the roots to the highest branches, of ten thousand fruits of palms upon ten thousand fruits.
6The grape-vines moreover had ten thousand plants.
7Moreover in the single vines there were ten thousand thousand bunches and in each of these a thousand single grapes: moreover these single trees bore a thousand fruits.
8And I said to the angel, Why does each tree bear a thousand fruits?
9The angel answered and said unto me, Because the Lord God gives an abounding flood of gifts to the worthy, because they also of their own
10will afflicted themselves when they were placed in the world doing all things on account of his holy name.
11And again I said to the angel, Sir, are these the only promises which the Most Holy God makes?
12And he answered and said to me: No! there are seven times greater than these.
13But I say unto thee that when the just go out of the body they shall see the promises and the good things which God has prepared for them.
14Till then, they shall sigh, and lament saying: Have we emitted any word from our mouth to vex our neighbour even on one day?
15I asked and said again: Are these alone the promises of God? And the angel answered and said unto me:
16These whom you now see are the souls of the married and those who kept the chastity of their nuptials, containing themselves.
17But to the virgins and those who hunger and thirst after righteousness and those who afflicted themselves for the sake of the name of
18God, God will give seven times greater than these, which I shall now show thee.
19And then he took me up from that place where I saw these things and behold, a river, and its waters were greatly whiter than milk, and I said to the angel, What is this?
20And he said to me: This is the Acherousian Lake where is the City of Christ, but not every man is permitted to enter that city;
21for this is the journey which leads to God, and if anyone is a fornicator and impious,
22and is converted and shall repent and do fruits worthy of repentance, at first indeed when he shall have gone out of the body,
23he is led and adores God, and thence by command of the Lord he is delivered to the angel Michael and he baptizes him in the Acherousian
24Lake—thus he leads them into the City of Christ alongside of those who have never sinned.
25But I wondered and blessed the Lord God for all the things which I saw.


Chapter 23

1And the angel answered and said unto me: Follow me and I will lead thee into the City of Christ.
2And he was standing on the Acherousian Lake and he put me into a golden ship and angels as it were three thousand were saying hymns before me till I arrived at the City of Christ.
3Moreover those who inhabited the City of Christ greatly rejoiced over me as I went to them, and I entered and saw the City of Christ, and it was all of gold, and twelve walls encircled it, and twelve interior towers, and each wall had between them single stadia in the circuit:
4And I said to the angel, Sir, how much is a stadium? The angel answered and said to me:
5As much as there is between the Lord God and the men who are on the earth, for the City of Christ is alone great.
6And there were twelve gates in the circuit of the city, of great beauty, and four rivers which encircled it.
7There was, moreover, a river of honey and a river of milk, and a river of wine and a river of oil.
8And I said to the angel: What are these rivers surrounding that city?
9And he saith to me: These are the four rivers which flow sufficiently for those who are in this land of promise, of which the names are:
10the river of honey is called Fison, and the river of milk Euphrates, and the river of oil Gion, and the river of wine Tigris, such
11therefore they are for those who when placed in the world did not use the power of these things,
12but they hungered for these things and afflicted themselves for the sake of the Lord God:
13so that when these enter into this city, the Lord will assign them these things on high above all measure.


Chapter 24

1I indeed entering the gates saw trees great and very high before the doors of the city, having no fruit but leaves only, and I saw a few men scattered in the midst of the trees, and they lamented greatly when they saw anyone enter the city.
2And those trees were sorry for them and humbled themselves and bowed down and again erected themselves.
3And I saw and wept with them and I asked the angel and said:
4Sir, who are these who are not admitted to enter into the City of Christ?
5And he said to me: These are they who zealously abstained day and night in fasts, but they had a proud heart above other men, glorifying and praising themselves and doing nothing for their neighbours.
6For they gave some friendly greeting, but to others they did not even say hail! and indeed they shewed hospitality to those only whom they wished, and if they did anything whatever for their neighbour they were immoderately puffed up.
7And I said: What then, Sir? Did their pride prevent them from entering into the City of Christ?
8And the angel answered and said unto me: Pride is the root of all evils.
9Are they better than the Son of God who came to the Jews with much humility?
10And I asked him and said: Why is it that the trees humble themselves and erect themselves again?
11And the angel answered and said to me: The whole time which these men passed on earth zealously serving God, on account of the confusion and reproaches of men at the time, they blushed and humiliated themselves, but they were not saddened nor did they repent that they should recede from their pride which was in them.
12This is why the trees humble themselves, and again are raised up. And I asked and said: For what cause were they admitted to the doors of the city?
13The angel answered and said unto me: Because of the great goodness of God, and because there is the entry of his holy men entering into this city:
14for this cause they are left in this place, but when Christ the King Eternal enters with his saints, as he enters just men may pray for these, and then they may enter into the city along with them:
15but yet none of them is able to have assurance such as they have who humbled themselves, serving the Lord God all their lives.


Chapter 25

1But I went on while the angel instructed me, and he carried me to the river of honey, and I saw there Isaiah and Jeremiah and Ezekiel and
2Amos, and Micah and Zechariah, the minor and major prophets, and they saluted me in the city.
3I said to the angel: What way is this?
4And he said to me: This is the way of the prophets, every one who shall have afflicted his soul and not done his own will because of God, when he shall have gone out of the world and have been led to the Lord God and adored him, then by the command of God he is handed
5over to Michael, and he leads him into the city to this place of the prophets, and they salute him as their friend and neighbour because he did the will of God.


Chapter 26

Again he led me where there is a river of milk, and I saw in that place all the infants whom Herod slew because of the name of Christ, and they saluted me, and the angel said to me:
All who keep their chastity with purity, when they shall have come out of the body, after they adore the Lord God are delivered to Michael and are led to the infants and they salute them, saying that they are our brothers and friends and members;
in themselves they shall inherit the promises of God.


Chapter 27

1Again he took me up and carried me to the north of the city and led me where there was a river of wine, and there I saw Abraham and Isaac and Jacob, Lot and Job and other saints, and they saluted me:
2and I asked and said: What is this place, my Lord?
3The angel answered and said to me:
4All who are receivers of pilgrims, when they go out of the world, first adore the Lord God, and are delivered to Michael and by this way are led into the city, and all the just salute him as son and brother, and say unto him:
5Because thou hast observed humanity and the receiving of pilgrims, come, have an inheritance in the city of the Lord our God:
6every just man shall receive good things of God in the city, according to his own action.


Chapter 28

1And again he carried me near the river of oil on the east of the city.
2And I saw there men rejoicing and singing psalms, and I said:
3Who are those, my Lord? And the angel saith to me:
4Those are they who devoted themselves to God with their whole heart and had no pride in themselves.
5For all those who rejoice in the Lord God and sing psalms to the Lord with their whole heart are here led into this city.


Chapter 29

1And he carried me into the midst of the city near the twelve walls.
2But there was in this place a higher wall, and I asked and said:
3Is there in the City of Christ a wall which in honour exceeds this place?
4And the angel answering said to me: There is a second better than the first, and similarly a third than the second, as each exceeds the other, unto the twelfth wall.
5And I said: Tell me, Sir, why one exceeds another in glory? And the angel answered and said unto me:
6All who have in themselves even a little detraction or zeal or pride, something of his glory would be made void even if he were in the city of Christ: look backward!
7And turning round I saw golden thrones placed in each gate, and on them men having golden diadems and gems:
8and I looked carefully and I saw inside between the twelve men thrones placed in another rank which appeared of much glory, so that no one is able to recount their praise.
9And I asked the angel and said: My lord, who is on the throne?
10And the angel answered and said unto me:
11Those thrones belong to those who had goodness and understanding of heart and made themselves fools for the sake of the Lord God, nor knew new Scriptures nor psalms, but, mindful of one chapter of the commands of God, and hearing what it contained they wrought thereby in much diligence and had a right zeal before the Lord God, and the admiration of them will seize all the saints in presence of the Lord God, for talking with one another they say,
12Wait and see the unlearned who know nothing more: by which means they merited so great and such a garment and so great glory on account of their innocence.
13And I saw in the midst of this city a great altar, very high, and there was one standing near the altar whose countenance shone as the sun, and he held in his hands a psaltery and harp, and he sang psalms, saying Halleluia!
14And his voice filled the whole city: at the same time when all they who were on the towers and gates heard him they responded Halleluia!
15so that the foundations of the city were shaken:
16and I asked the angel and said, Sir, who is this of so great power?
17And the angel said to me:
18This is David: this is the city of Jerusalem, for when Christ the King of Eternity shall come with the assurance of His kingdom, he again shall go before him that he may sing psalms, and all the just at the same time shall sing psalms responding Halleluia!
19And I said, Sir, how did David alone above the other saints make a beginning of psalm-singing?
20And the angel answered and said unto me:
21Because Christ the Son of God sits at the right hand of His Father, and this David sings psalms before him in the seventh heaven, and as is done in the heavens so also below, because the host may not be offered to God without David, but it is necessary that David should sing psalms in the hour of the oblation of the body and blood of Christ:
22as it is performed in heaven so also on earth.


Chapter 30

1And I said to the angel: Sir, what is Alleluia? And the angel answered and said to me:
2You ask questions about everything. And he said to me, Alleluia is said in the Hebrew language of God and angels, for the meaning of
3Alleluia is this: tecel cat. marith macha.
4And I said, Sir, what is tecel cat. marith macha?
5And the angel answered and said unto me: Tecel cat. marith macha is:
6Let us all bless him together.
7I asked the angel and said, Sir, do all who say Alleluia bless the Lord? And the angel answered and said to me:
8It is so, and again, therefore, if any one sing Alleluia and those who are present do not sing at the same time, they commit sin because they do not sing along with him.
9And I said: My lord, does he also sin if he be hesitating or very old?
10The angel answered and said unto me: Not so, but he who is able and does not join in the singing, know such as a despiser of the Word, and it would be proud and unworthy that he should not bless the Lord God his maker.


Chapter 31

1Moreover when he had ceased speaking to me, he led me outside the city through the midst of the trees and far from the places of the land of the good, and put me across the river of milk and honey:
2and after that he led me over the ocean which supports the foundations of heaven.
3The angel answered and said unto me: Dost thou understand why thou goest hence?
4And I said: Yes, sir. And he said to me: Come and follow me, and I will show thee the souls of the impious and sinners, that thou mayest know what manner of place it is.
5And I proceeded with the angel and he carried me by the setting of the sun, and I saw the beginning of heaven founded on a great river of water, and I asked:
6What is this river of water? And he said to me:
7This is Ocean which surrounds all the Earth.
8And when I was at the outer limit of Ocean I looked, and there was no light in that place, but darkness and sorrow and sadness: and I sighed.
9And I saw there a fervent river of fire, and in it a multitude of men and women immersed up to the knees, and other men up to the navel, others even up to the lips, others moreover up to the hair.
10And I asked the angel and said: Sir, who are those in the fiery river?
11And the angel answered and said to me:
12They are neither hot nor cold, because they were found neither in the number of the just nor in the number of the impious.
13For those spent the time of their life on earth passing some days in prayer, but others in sins and fornications, until their death.
14And I asked him and said: Who are these, Sir, immersed up to their knees in fire?
15He answered and said to me: These are they who when they have gone out of church throw themselves into strange conversations to dispute.
16Those indeed who are immersed up to the navel are those who, when they have taken the body and blood of Christ go and fornicate and did not cease from their sins till they died.
17Those who are immersed up to the lips are the detractors of each other when they assemble in the church of God: those up to the eyebrows are those who nod approval of themselves and plot spite against their neighbour.


Chapter 32

1And I saw on the north a place of various and diverse punishments full of men and women, and a river of fire ran down into it.
2Moreover I observed and I saw pits great in depth, and in them several souls together, and the depth of that place was as it were three thousand cubits, and I saw them groaning and weeping and saying:
3Have pity on us, O Lord! and none had pity on them.
4And I asked the angel and said: Who are these, Sir?
5And the angel answered and said unto me:
6These are they who did not hope in the Lord, that they would be able to have him as their helper.
7And I asked and said: Sir, if these souls remain for thirty or forty generations thus one upon another, if they were sent deeper, the pits I believe would not hold them.
8And he said to me: The Abyss has no measure, for beyond this it stretches down below him who is down in it:
9and so it is, that if perchance anyone should take a stone and throw it into a very deep well and after many hours it should reach the bottom, such is the abyss.
10For when the souls are thrown in there, they hardly reach the bottom in fifty years.


Chapter 33

1I, indeed, when I heard this, wept and groaned over the human race.
2The angel answered and said unto me: Why dost thou weep? Art thou more pitiful than God?
3For though God is good, He knows also that there are punishments, and He patiently bears with the human race, dismissing each one to work his own will in the time in which he dwells on the earth.


Chapter 34

1I further observed the fiery river and saw there a man being tortured by Tartaruchian angels having in their hands an iron with three hooks with which they pierced the bowels of that old man:
2and I asked the angel, and said: Sir, who is that old man on whom such torments are imposed?
3And the angel answered and said to me: He whom you see was a presbyter who did not perform well his ministry:
4when he had been eating and drinking and committing fornication he offered the host to the Lord at his holy altar.


Chapter 35

1And I saw not far away another old man led on by malign angels running with speed, and they pushed him into the fire up to his knees, and they struck him with stones and wounded his face like a storm,
2and did not allow him to say: Have pity on me!
3And I asked the angel and he said to me: He whom you see was a bishop, and did not perform well his episcopate, who indeed accepted the great name but did not enter into the witness of him who gave him the name in all his life, seeing that he did not do just judgment, and did not pity widows and orphans,
4but now he receives retribution according to his iniquity and his works.


Chapter 36

1And I saw another man in the fiery river up to his knees.
2Moreover his hands were stretched out and bloody, and worms proceeded from his mouth and nostrils and he was groaning and weeping, and crying he said:
3Have pity on me! for I am hurt above the rest who are in this punishment.
4And I asked, Sir, who is this? And he said to me:
5This man whom thou seest, was a deacon who devoured the oblations and committed fornications and did not right in the sight of God, for this cause he unceasingly pays this penalty.
6And I looked closely and saw alongside of him another man whom they delivered up with haste and cast into the fiery river, and he was (in it) up to the knees: and there came the angel who was set over the punishments having a great fiery razor, and with it he cut the lips of that man and the tongue likewise.
7And sighing, I lamented and asked: Who is that, sir.
8And he said to me, He whom thou seest was a reader and read to the people, but he himself did not keep the precepts of God: now he also pays the proper penalty.


Chapter 37

1And I saw another multitude of pits in the same place, and in the midst of it a river full of a multitude of men and women, and worms consumed them.
2But I lamented and sighing asked the angel and said: Sir, who are these?
3And he said to me: These are those who exacted interest on interest and trusted in their riches and did not hope in God that He was their helper.
4And after that I looked and saw another place, very narrow, and it was like a wall, and fire round about it.
5And I saw inside men and women gnawing their tongues, and I asked: Sir, who are these.
6And he said to me: These are they who in church disparage the Word of God, not attending to it, but as it were make naught of God and His angels:
7for that cause they now likewise pay the proper penalty.


Chapter 38

1And I observed and saw another old man down in a pit and his countenance was like blood, and I asked and said, Sir, what is this place?
2And he said to me: Into that pit stream all the punishments.
3And I saw men and women immersed up to the lips and I asked, Sir, who are these?
4And he said to me: These are the magicians who prepared for men and women evil magic arts and did not find how to stop them till they died.
5And again I saw men and women with very black faces in a pit of fire, and I sighed and lamented and asked, Sir, who are these?
6And he said to me: These are fornicators and adulterers who committed adultery having wives of their own:
7likewise also the women committed adultery having husbands of their own:
8therefore they unceasingly suffer penalties.


Chapter 39

1And I saw there girls having black raiment, and four terrible angels having in their hands burning chains, and they put them on the necks of the girls and led them into darkness:
2and I, again weeping, asked the angel: Who are these, Sir?
3And he said to me: These are they who, when they were virgins, defiled their virginity unknown to their parents; for which cause they unceasingly pay the proper penalties.
4And again I observed there men and women with hands cut and their feet placed naked in a place of ice and snow, and worms devoured them.
5But seeing them I lamented and asked: Sir, who are these?
6And he said to me: These are they who harmed orphans and widows and the poor, and did not hope in the Lord, for which cause they unceasingly pay the proper penalties.
7And I observed and saw others hanging over a channel of water, and their tongues were very dry, and many fruits were placed in their sight, and they were not permitted to take of them, and I asked:
8Sir, who are these? And he said to me: These are they who break their fast before the appointed hour, for this cause they unceasingly pay these penalties.
9And I saw other men and women hanging by their eyebrows and their hair, and a fiery river drew them, and I said:
10Who are these, my Lord? And he said to me: These are they who join themselves not to their own husbands and wives but to whores, and therefore they unceasingly pay the proper penalties.
11And I saw other men and women covered with dust, and their countenance was like blood, and they were in a pit of pitch and sulphur and running down into a fiery river, and I asked: Sir, who are these?
12And he said to me: These are they who committed the iniquity of Sodom and Gomorrah, the male with the male, for which reason they unceasingly pay the penalties.


Chapter 40

1And I observed and saw men and women clothed in bright garments, having their eyes blind, placed in a pit, and I asked:
2Sir, who are these?
3And he said to me: These are of the people who did alms, and knew not the Lord God, for which reason they unceasingly pay the proper penalties.
4And I observed and saw other men and women on an obelisk of fire, and beasts tearing them in pieces, and they were not allowed to say, Lord have pity on us!
5And I saw the angel of penalties putting heavy punishments on them and saying:
6Acknowledge the Son of God; for this was predicted to you, when the divine Scriptures were read to you, and you did not attend; for which cause God’s judgment is just, for your actions have apprehended you and brought you into these penalties.
7But I sighed and wept, and I asked and said:
8Who are these men and women who are strangled in fire and pay their penalties?
9And he answered me: These are women who defiled the image of God when bringing forth infants out of the womb, and these are the men who lay with them.
10And their infants addressed the Lord God and the angels who were set over the punishments, saying:
11Cursed be the hour to our parents, for they defiled the image of God, having the name of God but not observing His precepts:
12they gave us for food to dogs and to be trodden down of swine:
13others they threw into the river.
14But their infants were handed over to the angels of Tartarus who were set over the punishments, that they might lead them to a wide place of mercy:
15but their fathers and mothers were tortured in a perpetual punishment.
16And after that I saw men and women clothed with rags full of pitch and fiery sulphur, and dragons were coiled about their necks and shoulders and feet, and angels having fiery horns restrained them and smote them, and closed their nostrils, saying to them:
17Why did ye not know the time in which it was right to repent and serve God, and did not do it?
18And I asked: Sir, who are these?
19And he said to me: These are they who seem to give up the world for God, putting on our garb, but the impediments of the world made them wretched, not maintaining agapæ, and they did not pity widows and orphans:
20they did not receive the stranger and the pilgrim, nor did they offer the oblations, and they did not pity their neighbour.
21Moreover their prayer did not even on one day ascend pure to the Lord God, but many impediments of the world detained them, and they were
not able to do right in the sight of God, and the angels enclosed them in the place of punishments.
22Moreover they saw those who were in punishments and said to them:
23We indeed when we lived in the world neglected God, and ye also did likewise:
24as we also truly when we were in the world knew that ye were sinners.
25But ye said: These are just and servants of God, now we know why ye were called by the name of the Lord:
26for which cause they also pay their own penalties.
27And sighing I wept and said: Woe unto men, woe unto sinners! why were they born?
28And the angel answered and said unto me: Why dost thou lament?
29Art thou more pitiful than the Lord God who is blessed forever, who established judgment and sent forth every man to choose good and evil in his own will and do what pleases him?
30Then I lamented again very greatly, and he said to me:
31Dost thou lament when as yet thou hast not seen greater punishments?
32Follow me and thou shalt see seven times greater than these.


Chapter 41

1And he carried me south and placed me above a well, and I found it sealed with seven seals:
2and answering, the angel who was with me said to the angel of that place:
3Open the mouth of the well that Paul, the well-beloved of God, may see, for authority is given him that he may see all the pains of hell.
4And the angel said to me: Stand afar off that thou mayest be able to bear the stench of this place.
5When therefore the well was opened, immediately there arose from it a certain hard and malign stench, which surpasses all punishments:
6and I looked into the well and I saw fiery masses glowing in every part, and narrow places, and the mouth of the well was narrow so as to admit one man only.
7And the angel answered and said unto me: If any man shall have been put into this well of the abyss and it shall have been sealed over him, no remembrance of him shall ever be made in the sight of the Father and His Son and the holy angels.
8And I said: Who are these, Sir, who are put into this well?
9And he said to me: They are whoever shall not confess that Christ has come in the flesh and that the Virgin Mary brought him forth, and whoever says that the bread and cup of the Eucharist of blessing are not this body and blood of Christ.


Chapter 42

1And I looked to the south in the west and I saw there a restless worm and in that place there was gnashing of teeth:
2moreover the worms were one cubit long, and had two heads, and there I saw men and women in cold and gnashing of teeth.
3And I asked and said, Sir, who are these in this place?
4And he said to me: These are they who say that Christ did not rise from the dead and that this flesh will not rise again.
5And I asked and said: Sir, is there no fire nor heat in this place?
6And he said to me: In this place there is nothing else but cold and snow: and again he said to me:
7Even if the sun should rise upon them, they do not become warm on account of the superabundant cold of that place and the snow.
8But hearing these things I stretched out my hands and wept, and sighing again,
9I said: It were better for us if we had not been born, all of us who are sinners.


Chapter 43

1But when those who were in the same place saw me weeping with the angel, they themselves cried out and wept saying, Lord God have mercy upon us!
2And after these things I saw the heavens open, and Michael the archangel descending from heaven, and with him was the whole army of angels,
3and they came to those who were placed in punishment and seeing him, again weeping, they cried out and said, Have pity on us!
4Michael the archangel, have pity on us and on the human race, for on account of thy prayers the earth standeth.
5We now see the judgment and acknowledge the Son of God!
6It was impossible for us before these things to pray for this, before we entered into this place:
7for we heard that there was a judgment before we went out of the world, but impediments and the life of the world did not allow us to repent.
8And Michael answered and said: Hear Michael speaking! I am he who stands in the sight of God every hour:
9As the Lord liveth, in whose sight I stand, I do not intermit one day or one night praying incessantly for the human race, and I indeed pray for those who are on the earth:
10but they do not cease doing iniquity and fornications, and they do not bring to me any good while they are placed on earth:
11and ye have consumed in vanity the time in which ye ought to have repented.
12But I have always prayed thus and I now beseech that God may send dew and send forth rains upon the earth, and now I desire until the earth produce its fruits and verily I say, that if any have done but a little good, I will agonise for him, protecting him till he have escaped the judgment of penalties.
13Where therefore are your prayers? Where are your penances?
14Ye have lost your time contemptuously.
15But now weep and I will weep with you and the angels who are with me with the well-beloved Paul, if perchance the merciful God will have pity and give you refreshment.
16But hearing these words they cried out and wept greatly, and all said with one voice: Have pity on us, Son of God!
17And I, Paul, sighed and said: O Lord God! have pity on thy creature, have pity on the sons of men, have pity on thine image.


Chapter 44

1And I looked and saw the heaven move like a tree shaken by the wind.
2Suddenly, moreover, they threw themselves on their faces in the sight of the throne.
3And I saw twenty-four elders and twenty-four thousand adoring God, and I saw an altar and veil and throne, and all were rejoicing;
4and the smoke of a good odour was raised near the altar of the throne of God, and I heard the voice of one saying:
5For the sake of what do ye our angels and ministers intercede?
6And they cried out saying: We intercede seeing thy many kindnesses to the human race.
7And after these things I saw the Son of God descending from heaven, and a diadem was on his head.
8And seeing him those who were placed in punishment exclaimed all with one voice saying:
9Have pity, Son of the High God!
10Thou art He who shewest refreshment for all in the heavens and on earth, and on us likewise have pity, for since we have seen Thee, we have refreshment.
11And a voice went out from the Son of God through all the punishments saying:
12And what work have ye done that ye demand refreshment from me?
13My blood was poured out for your sakes, and not even so did ye repent:
14for your sakes I wore the crown of thorns on my head:
15for you I received buffets on my cheeks, and not even so did ye repent.
16I asked water when hanging on the cross and they gave me vinegar mixed with gall, with a spear they opened my right side, for my name’s sake they slew my prophets and just men, and in all these things I gave you a place of repentance and ye would not.
17Now, however, for the sake of Michael the archangel of my covenant and the angels who are with him,
18and because of Paul the well-beloved, whom I would not vex, for the sake of your brethren who are in the world and offer oblations,
19and for the sake of your sons, because my precepts are in them, and more for the sake of mine own kindness, on the day on which I rose from the dead, I give to you all who are in punishment a night and a day of refreshment forever.
20And they all cried out and said, We bless thee, Son of God, that Thou hast given us a night and a day of respite.
21For better to us is a refreshment of one day above all the time of our life which we were on earth, and if we had plainly known that this was intended for those who sin, we would have worked no other work, we would have done no business, and we would have done no iniquity:
22what need had we for pride in the world?
23For here our pride is crushed which ascended from our mouth against our neighbour:
24our plagues and excessive straitness and the tears and the worms which are under us, these are much worse to us than the pains which we have left behind us.
25When they said thus, the malign angels of the penalties were angered with them, saying:
26How long do ye lament and sigh? for ye had no pity.
27For this is the judgment of God who had no pity.
28But ye received this great grace of a day and a night’s refreshment on the Lord’s Day for the sake of Paul the well-beloved of God who descended to you.


Chapter 45

1And after that the angel said to me: Hast thou seen all these things?
2And I said: Yes, Sir.
3And he said to me: Follow me and I will lead thee into Paradise, that the just who are there may see thee, for lo! they hope to see thee, and they are ready to come to meet thee in joy and gladness.
4And I followed the angel by the impulse of the Holy Spirit, and he placed me in Paradise and said to me:
5This is Paradise in which Adam and his wife erred.
6Moreover I entered Paradise and saw the beginning of waters, and there was an angel making a sign to me and he said to me:
7Observe, said he, the waters, for this is the river of Physon which surrounds all the land of Evilla, and the second is Geon which
8surrounds all the land of Egypt and Ethiopia, and the third is Thigris which is over against the Assyrians, and another is Eufrates which waters all the land of Mesopotamia.
9And when I had gone inside I saw a tree planted from whose roots water flowed out, and from this beginning there were four rivers.
10And the spirit of God rested on that tree, and when the Spirit blew, the waters flowed forth, and I said: My Lord, is it this tree itself which makes the waters flow?
11And he said to me: That from the beginning, before the heavens and earth were manifested, and all things here invisible, the Spirit of
12God was borne upon the waters, but from the time when the command of God made the heavens and earth to appear, the Spirit rested upon this tree:
13wherefore whenever the Spirit blows, the waters flow forth from the tree.
14And he held me by the hand and led me near the tree of knowledge of good and evil, and he said:
15This is the tree by which death entered into the world, and receiving of it through his wife Adam ate and death entered into the world.
16And he shewed me another tree in the midst of Paradise, and saith to me:
17This is the tree of life.


Chapter 46

While I was yet looking upon the tree, I saw a virgin coming from afar and two hundred angels before her saying hymns, and I asked and said: Sir, who is she who comes in so great glory?
And he said to me: This is Mary the Virgin, the Mother of the Lord.
And coming near she saluted me and said: Hail, Paul! well-beloved of God and angels and men.
For all the saints prayed my Son Jesus who is my Lord that thou mightest come hither in the body that they might see thee before thou goest out of the world.
And the Lord said to them: Bear and be patient: yet a little and ye shall see him and he shall be with you for ever:
and again they all said to him together:
Do not vex us, for we desire to see him in the flesh, for by him Thy name was greatly glorified in the world, and we have seen that he endured all the labours whether of the greater or of the less.
This we learn from those who come hither.
For when we say: Who is he who directed you in the world?
they reply to us: There is one in the world whose name is Paul, he preaches and announces Christ, and we believe that many have entered into the kingdom through the virtue and sweetness of his speeches.
Behold all the just men are behind me coming to meet thee, Paul, and I first come for this cause to meet them who did the will of my Son and my Lord Jesus Christ,
I first advance to meet them and do not send them away to be as wanderers until they meet in peace.


Chapter 47

1When she had thus spoken, I saw three coming from afar, very beautiful in the likeness of Christ, and their forms were shining, and their angels, and I asked:
2Sir, who are these? And he said to me: Dost thou not know those?
3And I said: No, Sir. And he answered: These are the fathers of the people, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. And coming near they saluted me, and said:
4Hail, Paul, well-beloved of God and men; blessed is he who suffers violence for the Lord’s sake.
5And Abraham answered me and said: This is my son Isaac, and Jacob my well-beloved, and we have known the Lord and followed him;
6blessed are all they who believed in thy word, that they may be able to inherit the Kingdom of God by labour, by renunciation, and sanctification, and humility, and charity, and meekness, and right faith in the Lord;
7and we also have had devotion to the Lord whom thou preachest in the testament, that we might assist those who believed in him with their whole soul, and might minister unto them as fathers minister to their children.
8When they had thus spoken, I saw other twelve coming from afar in honour, and I asked: Sir, who are these?
9And he said: These are the patriarchs.
10And coming near they saluted me and said: Hail, Paul, well-beloved of God and men:
11the Lord did not vex us, that we might see thee yet in the body, before thou goest out of the world.
12And each one of them reminded me of his name in order, from Ruben to Benjamin: and Joseph said to me:
13I am he who was sold; but I say to thee, Paul, that all the things,
14whatever my brothers did to me, in nothing did I act maliciously with them, nor in all the labour which they imposed on me, nor in any point was I hurt by them on that account from morning till evening:
15blessed is he who receives some hurt on account of the Lord, and bears it, for the Lord will repay it to him manifold, when he shall have gone out of the world.


Chapter 48

1When he had spoken thus far, I saw another beautiful one coming from afar, and his angels saying hymns, and I asked:
2Sir, who is this that is beautiful of countenance?
3And he saith to me: Dost thou not know him?
4And I said: No, Sir. And he said to me: This is Moses the law-giver, to whom God gave the law.
5And when he had come near me, he immediately wept, and after that he saluted me: and I said to him:
6What dost thou lament? for I have heard that thou excellest every man in meekness.
7And he answered saying: I weep for those whom I planted with toil, because they did not bear fruit, nor did any profit by them; and I saw all the sheep whom I fed, that they were scattered and become as if they had no shepherd, and because all the toils which I endured for the sake of the sons of Israel were accounted as naught, and how greatsoever virtues I did in the midst of them these they did not understand,
8and I wonder that strangers and uncircumcised and idol-worshippers have been converted and have entered into the promises of God,
9but Israel has not entered; and now I say unto thee, brother Paul, that in that hour when the people hanged Jesus whom thou preachest,
10that the Father, the God of all, who gave me the law,
11and Michael and all the angels and archangels, and Abraham and Isaac, and Jacob, and all the just wept over the Son of God hanging on the cross.
12In that hour all the saints attended on me looking (upon me) and they said to me:
13See, Moses, what men of thy people have done to the Son of God.
14Wherefore thou art blessed, Paul, and blessed the generation and race which believed in thy word.


Chapter 49

1When he had spoken thus far, there came other twelve, and seeing me said:
2Art thou Paul the glorified in heaven and on earth? And I answered and said:
3What are ye? The first answered and said: I am Esaias whom Manasses cut asunder with a wooden saw.
4And the second said likewise: I am Jeremias who was stoned by the children of Israel and slain.
5And the third said: I am Ezekiel whom the children of Israel dragged by the feet over a rock in a mountain till they knocked out my brains, and we endured all these toils, wishing to save the children of Israel:
6and I say unto thee that after the toils which they laid upon me, I cast myself on my face in the sight of the Lord praying for them,
7bending my knees until the second hour of the Lord’s day, till Michael came and lifted me up from the earth.
8Blessed art thou, Paul, and blessed the nation which believed through thee.
9And as these passed by, I saw another, beautiful of countenance, and I asked:
10Sir, Who is this? Who when he had seen me, rejoiced and said to me:
11This is Lot who was found just in Sodom. And approaching he saluted me and said:
12Blessed art thou, Paul, and blessed the generation to which thou didst minister.
13And I answered and said to him: Art thou Lot who wast found just in Sodom?
14And he said: I entertained angels, as travellers, and when they of the city wished to violate them, I offered them my two virgin daughters who had not yet known men, and gave them to them saying:
15use them as ye will, but only to these men ye shall do no evil; for this cause they entered under the roof of my house.
16For this cause, therefore, we ought to be confident and know that if anyone shall have done anything, God shall repay him manifold when they shall come to him.
17Blessed art thou, Paul, and blessed the nation which believed in thy word.
18When, therefore, he had ceased talking to me, I saw another coming from a distance, very beautiful of countenance, and smiling, and his angels saying hymns:
19and I said to the angel who was with me: Has then each of the just an angel for companion?
20And he said to me: Each one of the saints has his own (angel) assisting him, and saying a hymn, and the one does not depart from the other.
21And I said: Who is this, Sir?
22And he said: This is Job. And approaching, he saluted me and said: Brother Paul, thou hast great praise with God and men.
23And I am Job, who laboured much for a period of thirty years from a plague in the blood; and verily in the beginning, the wounds which went forth from my body were like grains of wheat.
24But on the third day, they became as the foot of an ass; worms moreover which fell four digits in length:
25and on the third (day) the devil appeared and said to me:
26Say something against God and die. I said to him: If such be the will of God that I should remain under a plague all the time of my life till I die, I shall not cease from blessing the Lord, and I shall receive more reward.
27For I know that the labours of that world are nothing to the refreshment which is afterwards:
28for which cause blessed art thou, Paul, and blessed the nation which believed through thee.


Chapter 50

1When he had spoken thus far, another came calling from afar and saying:
2Blessed art thou, Paul, and blessed am I because I saw thee, the beloved of the Lord.
3And I asked the angel: Sir, who is this?
4And he answered and said unto me: This is Noe in the time of the deluge.
5And immediately we saluted each other: and greatly rejoicing he said to me:
6Thou art Paul the most beloved of God.
7And I asked him: Who art thou?
8And he said: I am Noe, who was in the time of the deluge.
9And I say to thee, Paul, that working for a hundred years, I made the ark, not putting off the tunic with which I was clad, nor did I cut the hair of my head.
10Till then also I cherished continence, not approaching my own wife: in those hundred years not a hair of my head grew in length, nor did my garments become soiled:
11and I besought men at all times saying: Repent, for a deluge of waters will come upon you.
12But they laughed at me, and mocked my words; and again they said to me:
13But this is the time of those who are able to play and sin freely, desiring her with whom it is possible to commit fornication frequently:
14for God does not regard this, and does not know what things are done by us men, and there is no flood of waters straightway coming upon this world.
15And they did not cease from their sins, till God destroyed all flesh which had the breath of life in it.
16Know then that God loveth one just man more than all the world of the impious.
17Wherefore, blessed art thou, Paul, and blessed is the nation which believes through thee.


Chapter 51

And turning round, I saw other just ones coming from afar, and I asked the angel:
Sir, who are those? And he answered me: These are Elias and Eliseus.
And they saluted me: and I said to them: Who are ye? And one of them answered and said:
I am Elias, the prophet of God; I am Elias who prayed, and because of my word, the heaven did not rain for three years and six months, on account of the unrighteousness of men.
God is just and true, who doeth the will of his servants: for the angels often besought the Lord for rain, and he said:
Be patient till my servant Elias shall pray and petition for this and I will send rain on the earth.
