Sacred Writing

"Every writings must be evaluated based on the truths they contain."

From the Nativity of the Virgin Mary to the death of patriarch Saint Joseph

Chapter 1

The Holy House of Nazareth

1The little house at Nazareth which Anne fitted up for Mary and Joseph, belonged to Anne.
2From her own dwelling, she could, unnoticed, reach it in about half an hour by a cross path.
3It lay not far from the gate.
4It had a small courtyard in front and nearby was a well, a couple of steps leading down to it.
5It was near a hill, but not built on it.
6A narrow path dug out of the hill separated it from the back of the house, in which there was one little window.
7It was darker on this side of the house than on the other.
8The back part was triangular and built on higher ground than the front.
9The foundations were cut in the rock; the upper part was a light masonry.
10Mary’s sleeping com-partment was in the back, and there it was that the angelic Annunciation took place.
11This chamber had a semicircular form, on account of the movable partitions placed around the walls and which were of coarser wickerwork than that ordinarily used for the light screens.
12The patterns in which these screens were woven were similar to wafers, and the colors used were designed to bring the figures out.
13Mary’s sleeping place was on the side just behind a wicker screen.
14On the left was a little closet with a small table and stool.
15This was the Blessed Virgin’s oratory.
16This back room was separated from the rest of the house by a fireplace, which consisted of a graded wall from whose center over the slightly raised hearth, a chimney rose up to the roof and ended in a tube above it.
17Over the opening through which the tube projected was built a little roof.
18On top of the chimney, I saw in after years two little bells hanging.
19To the right and left of the chimney and opening into Mary’s rooms were doors up to which three steps led.
20In the chim-ney wall were all kinds of nooks in which stood the little vessels that I still see at Loretto.
21Behind was a rafter of cedar wood, upon which the wall of the chimney rested.
22From this upright rafter ran a crossbeam to the center of the back wall, and into this there were others dovetailed from the two side walls.
23These beams were of a bluish cast with yellow ornaments.
24Between them one could see up through the roof, which was hung with large leaves and matting, and in three places, namely in the three corners, adorned with stars.
25The star in the middle corner was large like the morning star.
26Later on the ceiling was adorned with numerous stars.
27Over the horizontal rafter, which extended from the chimney to the back of the wall, was an opening in the center for the window, and under this was hung a lamp.
28There was a rafter under the chimney also.
29The roof was not high and pointed, but so level that one might walk around the edge.
30It was flat on top, and there rose the chimney with its tubes, protected by the little roof.
31When after Joseph’s death the Blessed Virgin removed to the neighborhood of Capharnaum, the Holy House was left beautifully adorned like a sacred shrine.
32Mary often went from Capharnaum to visit the scene of the Incarnation and to pray there.
33Peter and John, whenever they went to Palestine, visited the House of Nazareth and celebrated Mass in it.
34An altar was erected where the fireplace used to be.
35The little cupboard once used by Mary was placed as a tabernacle upon the altar.
36I have often in vision witnessed the transporting of the Holy House to Loretto.
37For a long time, I could not believe it, and yet I continued to see it.
38I saw the Holy House borne over the sea by seven angels.
39It had no foundation, but there was under it a shining surface of light.
40On either side was something like a handle.
41Three angels carried it on one side and three on the other; the seventh hovered in front of it, a long train of light after him.
42I remember that it was the back of the house, the part that contained the fireplace, the altar of the Apostles, and the little window, that was transported to Europe.
43It seems to me when I recall it that the rest of the building was in some danger of falling.
44I see in Loretto the Crucifix also that the Blessed Virgin had when in Ephesus.
45It was formed of different kinds of wood.
46Later on, it came into the possession of the Apostles.
47Many miracles take place before that Crucifix.
48The wall of the Holy House of Loretto is entirely the original one.
49Even the rafter under the chimney is still in its place.
50The miraculous picture of the Mother of God stands on the altar of the Apostles.


Chapter 2

Mary’s Annunciation

1On the day upon which the Church celebrates the feast, I had a vision of Mary’s Annunciation.
2I saw the Blessed Virgin a short time after her marriage in the house of Nazareth.
3Joseph was not there.
4He was at that moment journeying with two beasts of burden on the road to Tiberias, whither he was going to get his tools.
5But Anne was in the house with her maid and two of the virgins who had been with Mary in the Temple.
6Everything in the house had been newly arranged by Anne.
7Toward evening, they all prayed standing around a circular stool from which they afterward ate vegetables that had been served.
8Anne seemed to be very busy about the household affairs, and for a time she moved around here and there, while the Blessed Virgin ascended the steps to her room.
9There she put on a long, white, woolen garment, such as it was customary to wear during prayer, a girdle around her waist, and a yellowish-white veil over her head.
10The maid entered, lighted the branched lamp, and retired.
11Mary drew out a little, low table, which stood folded by the wall, and placed it in the center of the room.
12It had a semicircular leaf, which could be raised on a movable support so that when ready for use the little table stood on three legs.
13Mary spread upon it a red and then a white, transparent cover, which hung down on the side opposite the leaf.
14It was fringed at the end and embroidered in the center.
15A white cover was spread on the rounded edge.
16When the little table was prepared, Mary laid a small, round cushion before it and, resting both hands on the leaf, she gently sank on her knees, her back turned to her couch, the door of the chamber to her right.
17The floor was carpeted.
18Mary lowered her veil over her face, and folded her hands, but not the fingers, upon her breast.
19I saw her praying for a long time with intense fervor.
20She prayed for Redemption, for the promised King, and that her own supplications might have some influence upon His coming.
21She knelt long, as if in ecstasy, her face raised to Heaven; then she drooped her head upon her breast and thus continued her prayer.
22And now she glanced to the right and beheld a radiant youth with flowing, yellow hair.
23It was the archangel Gabriel.
24His feet did not touch the ground.
25In an oblique line and surrounded by an effulgence of light and glory, he came floating down to Mary.
26The lamp grew dim, for the whole room was lighted up by the glory.
27The angel, with hands gently raised before his breast, spoke to Mary.
28I saw the words like letters of glittering light issuing from his lips.
29Mary replied, but without looking up.
30Then the angel again spoke and Mary, as if in obedience to his command, raised her veil a little, glanced at him, and said, “Behold the handmaid of the Lord.
31May it be done unto me according to thy word!” I saw her now in deeper ecstasy.
32The ceiling of the room vanished, and over the house appeared a luminous cloud with a pathway of light leading up from it to the opened heavens.
33Far up in the source of this light, I beheld a vision of the Most Holy Trinity.
34It was like a triangle of glory, and I thought that I saw therein the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost.
35As Mary uttered the words: “May it be done unto me according to thy word!” I saw an apparition of the Holy Ghost.
36The countenance was human and the whole apparition environed by dazzling splendor, as if surrounded by wings.
37From the breast and hands, I saw issuing three streams of light.
38They penetrated the right side of the Blessed Virgin and united into one under her heart.
39At that instant Mary became perfectly transparent and luminous.
40It was as if opacity disappeared like darkness before that flood of light.
41While the angel and with him the streams of glory vanished, I saw down the path of light that led up to Heaven, showers of half-blown roses and tiny green leaves falling upon Mary.
42She, entirely absorbed in self, saw in herself the Incarnate Son of God, a tiny, human form of light with all the members, even to the little fingers perfect.
43It was about midnight that I saw this mystery.
44Some time elapsed, and then Anne and the other women entered Mary’s room, but when they beheld her in ecstasy they immediately withdrew.
45The Blessed Virgin then arose, stepped to the little altar on the wall, let down the picture of a swathed child that was rolled above it, and prayed standing under the lamp before it.
46Only toward morning did she lie down.
47Mary was at this time a little over fourteen years old.
48An intuitive knowledge of what had taken place was conferred upon Anne.
49Mary knew that she had conceived the Redeemer, yes, her interior lay open before her, and so she already understood that her Son’s kingdom should be a supernatural one, and that the House of Jacob, the Church, would be the reunion of regenerate mankind.
50She knew that the Redeemer would be the King of His people, that He would purify them and render them victorious; but that in order to redeem them He must suffer and die.
51It was explained to me likewise why the Redeemer remained nine months in His mother’s womb, why He was born a little child and not a perfect man like Adam, and why also He did not take the beauty of Adam in Paradise.
52The Incarnate Son of God willed to be conceived and born that conception and birth, rendered so very unholy by the Fall, might again become holy.
53Mary was His Mother, and He did not come sooner because Mary was the first and the only woman conceived without sin.
54Jesus when put to death was thirty-three years, four months, and two weeks old.
55I thought all the while: Here in Nazareth, things are different from what they are in Jerusalem.
56There the women dare not set foot in the Temple, but here in this church at Nazareth, a virgin is herself the Temple and the Most Holy rests in her.


Chapter 3

Mary’s Visitation

1Mary’s Annunciation took place before Joseph’s return.
2He had not yet settled at Nazareth when, with Mary, he started on the journey to Hebron.
3After the Conception of Jesus, the Blessed Virgin experienced a great desire to visit her cousin Elizabeth.
4I saw her travelling with Joseph toward the south.
5Once I saw her passing the night in a hut made of wickerwork and which was all overrun with vines and beautiful white blossoms.
6From that point to Zachary’s house, it was a journey of about twelve hours.
7Near Jerusalem they turned off to the north in order to take a more solitary route.
8They made the circuit of a little city two leagues from Emmaus, and took a road traversed by Jesus in after years.
9Although it was a long journey, they made it very quickly.
10They now had to cross two hills.
11I saw them resting between them, eating some bread and refreshing themselves with some balsam drops which they had collected on the way, and which they mingled with their drinking water.
12The hill was formed of overhanging rocks and caves.
13The valleys were very fertile.
14I remarked on the road one particular flower.
15It had fine green leaves and a cluster of nine tiny bell-shaped blossoms, white, lightly flushed with red.
16Mary wore a brown, woolen under dress over which was a gray one with a girdle, and a yellowish covering on her head.
17Joseph carried in a bundle a long brownish garment with a cowl, and bands in front.
18It was one that Mary was accustomed to wear whenever she went either to the Temple or the synagogue.
19Zachary’s house stood upon a solitary hill, and other dwellings were scattered around.
20Not far from it, a tolerably large brook flowed down from the mountain.
21Elizabeth had learned in vision that one of her race was to give birth to the Messiah; she had dwelt in thought upon Mary, had very greatly desired to see her, and had indeed beheld her journeying to Hebron.
22In a little room, to the right of the entrance to the house, she placed seats, and here she tarried, often looking long and anxiously down the road, in the hope of catching the first glimpse of Mary.
23When Zachary was returning from the Passover, I saw Elizabeth, urged by an impetuous desire, hurrying from the house and going a considerable distance on the road to Jerusalem.
24When Zachary met her, he was alarmed to find her so far from home and that, too, in her present condition.
25But she told him of her anxiety and that she could not help thinking that her cousin Mary was corning from Nazareth to see her.
26Zachary, however, thought it improbable that the newly married couple would at that time undertake so great a journey.
27On the following day, I saw Elizabeth taking the road again under the influence of the same impression, and now I saw the Holy Family coming to meet her.
28Elizabeth was advanced in years.
29She was tall, her face small and delicate, and she wore something wrapped around her head.
30She was acquainted with Mary only by hearsay.
31As soon as the Blessed Virgin saw Elizabeth, she knew her and hurried on to meet her, while Joseph purposely held back.
32Mary had already reached the houses in the neighborhood of Zachary’s home.
33Their occupants were enraptured at her beauty, and filled with such reverence by her demeanor that they stood back modestly.
34When the cousins met, they saluted each other joyfully with outstretched hands.
35I saw a light in Mary and issuing from her a ray which entered into Elizabeth, who thereby became wonderfully agitated.
36They did not pause long in sight of the beholders, but arm in arm passed up the courtyard to the door of the house, where Elizabeth once more bade Mary welcome.
37Joseph went around to the side of the house and into an open hall where sat Zachary.
38He respectfully saluted the aged priest, who responded in writing on his tablet.
39Mary and Elizabeth entered the room in which was the fireplace.
40Here they embraced, clasping each other in their arms and pressing cheek to cheek.
41I saw light streaming down between them.
42Then it was that Elizabeth, becoming interiorly inflamed, stepped back with uplifted hands, and exclaimed, “Blessed art thou among women, and blessed is the Fruit of thy womb.
43“And whence is this to me, that the Mother of my Lord should come to me?
44“For behold, as soon as the voice of thy salutation sounded in my ears, the infant in my womb leaped for joy.
45“And blessed art thou that hast believed, because those things shall be accomplished that were spoken to thee by the Lord.”
46At these last words, Elizabeth took Mary into the little room prepared for her that she might sit down and rest.
47It was only a few steps from where they then were.
48Mary released her hold upon Elizabeth’s arm, crossed her hands on her breast, and divinely inspired, uttered her canticle of thanksgiving: “My soul doth magnify the Lord, and my spirit hath rejoiced in God, my Saviour.
49“Because He hath regarded the humility of His handmaid; for behold from henceforth all genera-tions shall call me blessed.
50“Because He that is mighty hath done great things to me: and holy is His name.
51“And His mercy is from generation unto generations, to them that fear Him.
52“He hath showed might in His arm: He hath scattered the proud in the conceit of their heart.
53“He hath put down the mighty from their seat, and hath exalted the humble.
54“He hath filled the hungry with good things: and the rich He hath sent empty away.
55“He hath received Israel his servant, being mindful of His mercy.
56“As He spoke to our fathers, to Abraham, and to His seed forever.
” 57I saw Elizabeth, moved by similar emotion, reciting the whole canticle with Mary.
58Then they seated themselves on low seats.
59A small goblet was on the little table.
60And, oh, I was so happy! I sat nearby and prayed with them the whole time.
61I saw Joseph and Zachary still together.
62They were conversing by means of the tablet, and always about the coming of the Messiah.
63Zachary was a tall, handsome old man clothed like a priest.
64He and Joseph sat together at the side of the house that opened on the garden, in which Mary and Elizabeth were now sitting on a rug under a high, spreading tree.
65Behind the tree was a fountain from which gushed water when a spigot was pressed.
66I saw grass and flowers around, and trees bearing little, yellow plums.
67Mary and Elizabeth were eating rolls and small fruits out of Joseph’s travelling pouch.
68What touching simplicity and moderation! Two maids and two men servants were in the house.
69They prepared a table under the tree.
70Joseph and Zachary came out and ate something.
71Joseph wanted to return home at once, but they persuaded him to stay eight days.
72He knew not of Mary’s conception.
73The women were silent on that subject.
74They had a secret understanding together about their interior sentiments.
75When all, Mary and Elizabeth, Joseph and Zachary, were together, they prayed making use of a kind of litany.
76I saw a cross appear in their midst, and still there was no cross at that time.
77Yes, it was as if two crosses visited each other.
78In the evening they all sat together again in the garden near a lamp under the tree.
79A cover like a tent was stretched under the tree, and low stools with backs stood around.
80After that I saw Joseph and Zachary going to an oratory, while Mary and Elizabeth retired to their little chamber.
81They were inflamed with divine ardor, and together they recited the Magnificat.
82The Blessed Virgin wore a transparent white veil which she lowered when speaking to men.
83Zachary took Joseph on the following day to another garden at some distance from the house.
84He was in all things most exact and methodical.
85This second garden was set out with beautiful bushes and trees full of fruit.
86In the center was an avenue of trees, and at the end of it a small house whose entrance was on the side.
87Above were openings with slides like windows.
88A woven couch filled with moss or some other fine plant, stood in one room in which there were also two white figures as large as children.
89I have no clear knowledge of how they came there nor what they signified, but they appeared to me to be very like Zachary and Elizabeth, only much younger.
90I saw Mary and Elizabeth much together.
91Mary helped with everything around the house and pre-pared all kinds of necessaries for the child.
92Both she and Elizabeth knit on a large coverlet for the latter, and they worked also for the poor.
93During Mary’s absence, Anne frequently sent her maid to see after Mary’s house at Nazareth, and once I saw her there herself.
94I saw Zachary and Joseph spending the night of the next day in the garden at some distance from the house.
95They slept part of the time in the little summer house, and prayed during the other part in the open air.
96They returned quite early in the morning to the house where Mary and Elizabeth had passed the night.
97Mary and Elizabeth recited together morning and evening the hymn of thanksgiving, the Magnificat, which Mary had received from the Holy Ghost at the salutation of Elizabeth.
98During its recital they stood opposite each other against the wall, as if in choir, their hands crossed upon their breast, the black veil of each covering her face.
99At the second part, which refers to God’s promise, I saw the previous history of the Most Holy Incarnation and the mystery of the Most Holy Sacrament of the Altar, from Abraham down to Mary.
100I saw Abraham sacrificing Isaac, also the Mystery of the Ark of the Covenant, which Moses received on the night before the departure from Egypt, and by which he was enabled to escape and conquer.
101I recognized its connection with the holy Incarnation, and it seemed to me as if this Mystery were now fulfilled or living in Mary.
102I saw also the Prophet Isaias and his prophecy of the Virgin, and from him to Mary visions of the approach of the Most Blessed Sacrament.
103I still remember that I heard the words: “From father to father down to Mary, there are more than fourteen generations.”
104I saw also Mary’s blood taking its rise in her ancestors and flowing nearer and nearer to the Incarnation.
105I have no words to describe this clearly.
106I can say only that I saw, sometimes here, sometimes there, the people of different races.
107There seemed to issue from them a beam of light which always terminated in Mary as she appeared at that moment with Elizabeth.
108I saw this beam issuing first from the Mystery of the Ark of the Covenant and ending in Mary.
109Then I saw Abraham and from him a ray, which again ended in Mary, etc.
110Abraham must have dwelt quite near to Mary’s abode at that time; for during the Magnificat I saw that the beam which proceeded from him came from no great distance, while those from persons nearer to the Mother of God in point of time seemed to come from afar.
111Their rays were as fine, as clear as those of the sun when they shine through a narrow opening.
112In such a beam, I beheld Mary’s blood glancing red and bright, and it was said to me: “Behold, as pure as this red light must the blood of that Virgin be from whom the Son of God will become incarnate.”
113Once I saw Mary and Elizabeth going in the evening to Zachary’s country place.
114They took with them rolls and fruit in little baskets, for they intended to stay overnight.
115Joseph and Zachary followed them later.
116I saw Mary going to meet them as they entered.
117Zachary had brought his little tablet, but it was too dark for writing.
118I saw Mary speaking to him.
119She was telling him that he should speak on that night.
120He laid aside his tablet and conversed orally with Joseph.
121I saw all this to my own great astonishment.
122Then my guide said to me: “Why, what is that?” and he showed me a vision of St.
123Goar, who hung his mantle on the sunbeams as on a hook.
124I received then the instruction that lively, childlike confidence makes all things real and substantial.
125These two expressions gave me great interior light upon all kinds of miracles, but I cannot explain it.
126They, Mary, Elizabeth, Joseph, and Zachary, all spent the night in the garden.
127They sat or walked two by two, prayed now and then, or retired into the little summer house to rest.
128I heard them say that Joseph would return home on the evening of the Sabbath, and that Zachary would go with him as far as Jerusalem.
129The moon shone bright in a starry sky.
130I t was indescribably calm and lovely near those holy souls.
131Once also I had a peep into Mary’s little chamber.
132It was night, and she was at rest.
133She was lying on her side with one hand under her head.
134Over her brown under-dress she wound from head to foot a strip of white, woolen stuff about an ell in width.
135When preparing for rest, she took one end of this strip under her arm and wound it tightly around her head and the upper part of her person, then down to the feet and up again; so that she was entirely enveloped, and could not take a long step.
136She did this near the couch, at the head of which was a little roll of something for a pillow.
137The arms from the elbow down were left free, and the veiling of the head opened on the breast.
138I often saw under Mary’s heart a glory in whose center burned an indescribably clear little flame, and over Elizabeth’s womb a similar glory, but the light in it was not so clear.
139When the Sabbath began, I saw in Zachary’s house, in a room that I had not before seen, lamps lighted and the Sabbath celebrated.
140Zachary, Joseph, and about six other men from the neighborhood were standing and praying under a lamp and around a little chest upon which lay rolls of writing.
141They had on their heads something like a small veil.
142They did not make so many distorted movements of the body as do the latter-day Jews, although like them they frequently bowed the head and raised the arms.
143Mary, Elizabeth, and two other women stood apart in a grated partition from which they could see into the oratory.
144They were entirely enveloped, their prayer mantles over their heads.
145Zachary wore his festive robes the whole of the Sabbath.
146They consisted of a long, white garment with rather narrow sleeves.
147He was girdled with a broad cincture, wound many times around him.
148On it were letters, and from it hung straps.
149This garment was provided with a cowl, which hung in plaits from the head down the back like a folded veil.
150When he moved or performed any action, he threw this garment rolled together with the ends of the girdle up over one shoulder, and stuck it into the girdle under his arm.
151His lower limbs were loosely bound, and the strip enveloping them fastened by the straps that kept the soles in place upon his naked feet.
152He showed his priestly mantle to Joseph.
153It was sleeveless, wide and heavy and very beautiful, flashing with white and purple intermixed.
154It was closed on the breast with three jeweled clasps.
155When the Sabbath was over, I saw them eating again for the first time.
156They took their repast together under the trees in the garden near the house.
157They ate green leaves previously dipped into something, and sucked little bunches of herbs which too had been soaked.
158There were little bowls of small fruits on the table and other dishes, from which they partook of something with brown, transparent spatulas.
159It may have been honey that they were eating with horn spatulas.
160There were also little rolls, and I saw them eating them.
161After the meal, Joseph accompanied by Zachary started on his journey home.
162The night was calm, the moon shining, and the sky studded with stars.
163Before parting, all prayed separately.
164Joseph took with him his little bundle in which were a few rolls and a small jug of something.
165Both the travelers had staves; but Joseph’s was hooked on top, while Zachary’s was long and ended in a knob.
166Both had travelling mantles which they wore over their head.
167Before starting, they embraced Mary and Elizabeth, alternately pressing them to their heart.
168But I saw no kissing at that time.
169The parting was calm and cheerful.
170The two women accompanied them a short distance, and then the travelers proceeded alone.
171The night was unspeakably lovely.
172Mary and Elizabeth now returned to the house and went into Mary’s chamber.
173A lamp was burning upon a bracket on the wall, as was usual while Mary slept or prayed.
174The two women stood facing each other, and recited the Magnificat.
175They spent the whole night in prayer, for what reason I cannot now say.
176Through the day I saw Mary busy with all kinds of work, weaving covers, for instance.
177I saw Joseph and Zachary still on the road.
178They spent the night under a shed.
179They took very cir-cuitous roads and, I think, visited many people, for they were three days on their journey.
180Again I saw Joseph at Nazareth.
181Anne’s maid took charge of the house for him, going to and fro between the two houses.
182With this exception, Joseph was entirely alone.
183I also saw Zachary returning home, and I saw Mary and Elizabeth reciting as usual the Magnificat, and doing all kinds of work.
184Toward evening, they used to walk in the garden.
185There was a well in it, a rare occurrence in this part of the country; therefore travelers always took with them in a little jug some kind of juice to drink.
186Sometimes also, and generally toward evening when it grew cool, Mary and Elizabeth walked some distance from the house, for it stood alone in the midst of fields.
187They usually retired about nine o’clock, and always rose again before the sun.
188The Blessed Virgin remained with Elizabeth three months, until after the birth of John, but she returned to Nazareth before his circumcision.
189Joseph went to meet her halfway on the journey, and for the first time noticed that she was pregnant.
190But he gave no sign of his knowledge, and struggled with his doubts.
191Mary, who had feared this, was silent and preoccupied, thus increasing his uneasiness.
192When arrived in Nazareth, Mary went to the parents of the deacon Parmenas and remained some days with them, Joseph’s anxiety had meanwhile increased to such a degree that, when Mary returned home, he determined to flee from the house.
193Then the angel appeared to him and consoled him.


Chapter 4

Feast Pictures

1I saw a wonderful and almost indescribable vision of a feast.
2I saw a church that looked like a slender, delicate, octangular fruit, the roots of whose stem touched the earth over a bubbling fountain.
3The stem was not high, one could just see between the church and the earth.
4The entrance was over the spring which bubbled and bubbled, casting out something white like earth or sand, and rendering all around green and fruitful.
5There were no roots over the spring in front of the church.
6The center of the interior was like the capsule in an apple, the cells formed of many delicate white threads.
7In these cells were little organs like the kernels of an apple.
8Through an opening in the floor, one could look straight down into the bubbling spring.
9I saw some kernels that looked withered and decayed, falling into it.
10But while I gazed, the fruit seemed to be developing more and more into a church; and the capsule at last appeared something like a piece of machinery, like a loose artificial nosegay in the center of it.
11And now I saw the Blessed Virgin and Elizabeth standing on that nosegay and looking again like two tabernacles, the one the tabernacle of a saint, the other that of the Most Holy.
12The two blessed women turned toward each other and offered mutual felicitations.
13Then there issued from them two figures, Jesus and John.
14John, the larger of the two, lay coiled on the earth, his head in his lap; but Jesus was like a tiny child formed of light, just as I so often see Him in the Blessed Sacrament.
15Upright and hovering, He moved toward John and passed over him like a white vapor as he lay there with his face upon the earth.
16The reflection from the snowy vapor glanced through the opening in the floor down into the spring, and by it was swallowed up.
17Then Jesus raised the little John and embraced him, after which each returned to the womb of his mother, who meantime had been singing the Magnificat.
18I saw also during that singing, Joseph and Zachary issuing from the walls on opposite sides of the church and followed by an ever-increasing flow of people, while the whole building continued unfolding, as it were, taking more and more the appearance of a church and the occasion that of a sacred festival.
19Vines with luxuriant foliage were growing around the church, and they became so dense that they had to be trimmed.
20The church now rested on the earth.
21In it was an altar, and through an opening over the bubbling spring arose a baptismal font.
22Many people entered by the door, and there was at last a grand and perfect festival.
23All that took place therein, both in form and in action, was a silent growth.
24I cannot relate all; words fail me.
25On John’s feast, I had another vision of a festival.
26The octangular church was transparent, as if formed of crystal or jets of water.
27In the center was a well spring above which arose a little tower.
28I saw John standing by it and baptizing.
29The vision changed.
30Out of the spring grew a flower stalk, around which arose eight pillars supporting a pyramidal crown.
31Upon the crown stood the grandparents of Anne, Elizabeth, and Joseph; a little distant from the main stem were Mary and Joseph with the parents of the latter and those of Zachary.
32Up on the central stem stood John.
33A voice seemed to proceed from him, and I saw nations and kings entering the church and receiving the Blessed Eucharist from the hands of a Bishop.
34I heard John saying that their happiness was greater than his.


Chapter 5

The Blessed Virgin’s Preparations for the Birth of Christ. Journey to Bethlehem

1I saw the Blessed Virgin for many days with Anne, while Joseph remained alone in Nazareth, one of Anne’s maids taking charge of the house for him.
2They, Mary and Joseph, received their principal support from Anne’s house as long as she lived.
3I saw the Blessed Virgin near Anne sewing and embroidering bands and tapestry.
4They seemed to be very busy in the house.
5Joachim must long since have been dead, for I saw Anne’s second husband there and a little girl of from six to seven years old.
6She was helping Mary and being taught by her.
7If not a daughter of Anne, it must have been one of Mary Cleophas’s children also called Mary.
8I saw Mary sitting in a room with other women and preparing covers large and small.
9Some were embroidered with gold and silver.
10There was one large coverlet in a box in the midst of the women, at which all were working, knitting with two little wooden needles and balls of colored wool.
11Anne was very busy.
12She went around from one to another, receiving and giving wool.
13All expected Mary to be delivered in Anne’s house, and these covers and other things were being prepared partly for the birth of the Child and partly as gifts for the poor.
14Everything was of the best, and all abundantly, and richly provided.
15They knew not that Mary would, of necessity, have to journey to Bethlehem.
16Joseph was at that moment on his way to Jerusalem with cattle for sacrifice.


Chapter 5

John never received the Blessed Sacrament.

1I saw Joseph returned from Jerusalem. He had taken thither cattle for sacrifice, and had put up at the house before the Bethlehem gate.
2It was at this same inn that he and Mary stopped later on, before Mary’s Purification.
3The keeper of the inn was an Essenian.
4Joseph went from there to Bethlehem, but did not visit his relatives.
5He was looking around after a place to build, also for some means of procuring lumber and tools, for in the spring after Mary’s delivery, which he thought would take place in Nazareth, he intended to remove with her to Bethlehem, as he did not care for Nazareth.
6He wanted to get a place near the inn of the Essenian.
7From Bethlehem he went again to Jerusalem, to offer sacrifice.
8When he was returning from this journey to Jerusalem, and about midnight was crossing the field of Chimki, six hours from Nazareth, an angel appeared to him and said that he should set out at once with Mary for Bethlehem, as it was there that her Child was to be born.
9The angel told him, moreover, that he should provide himself with a few necessaries, but no laces nor embroidered covers, and he mentioned all the other things he was to take.
10Joseph was very much surprised.
11He was told also that, besides the ass upon which Mary was to ride, he was to take with him a little she-ass of one year which had not yet foaled.
12This little animal they were to let run at large, and then follow the road it would take.
13I saw Joseph and Mary in their house at Nazareth; Anne too was present.
14Joseph informed them of the commands he had received, and they began to prepare for the journey.
15Anne was very much troubled about it.
16The Blessed Virgin had had all along an interior admonition that she should bring forth her child in Bethlehem; but in her humility she had kept silence.
17She knew it, also, from the Prophecies.
18She had all the Prophecies referring to the birth of the Messiah in her little closet at Nazareth; she read them very often and prayed for their fulfillment.
19She had received them from her teachers at the Temple, and by the same holy women had been instructed upon them.
20Her prayer was always for the coming of the Messiah.
21She esteemed her happy of whom the Child should be born, and she desired to serve her as her lowest handmaid.
22In her humility, she had never conceived the thought that she herself was to be the one.
23From those Prophecies she knew that the Saviour would be born in Bethlehem, therefore she lovingly submitted to the Divine Will and began her journey.
24It was a very painful one for her, since at that season it was cold among the mountains.
25Mary had an inexpressible feeling that henceforth she must and could be only poor.
26She could possess no exterior goods, for she had all in herself.
27She knew that she was to be the Mother of the Son of God.
28She knew and she felt that, as by a woman sin had entered into the world, so now by a woman the Expiation was to be born.
29It was under the influence of this feeling that she had exclaimed: “Behold the handmaid of the Lord!” I understood, likewise, that Jesus was conceived of the Holy Ghost about the hour of midnight, and about midnight should be born.
30I saw Joseph and Mary with Anne, Mary Cleophas, and some servants silently setting out upon their journey.
31They started from Anne’s.
32An ass bore a comfortable cross-seat for Mary and her baggage.
33On the field of Chimki, where the angel had appeared to Joseph, Anne had a pasture ground; and here the servants went to get the little she-ass of one year which Joseph had to take with him.
34She ran after the Holy Family.
35Anne, Mary Cleophas, and the servants now parted from Joseph and Mary after a touching leave-taking.
36I saw the two travelers going some distance further and putting up at a house that lay on very high ground.
37They were well received.
38I think the proprietor was the lease holder of a farm called the House of Chimki and to which the field belonged.
39From it one could see far into the distance, yes, even to the mountains near Jerusalem.
40I again saw the Holy Family in a very cold valley, through which they were making their way toward a mountain.
41The ground was covered with frost and snow.
42It was about four hours from the House of Chimki.
43Mary was suffering exceedingly from the cold.
44She halted near a pine tree, and exclaimed: “We must rest.
45I can go no farther.”
46 Joseph arranged a seat for her under the tree, in which he placed a light.
47I often saw that done at night by travelers in those parts.
48The Blessed Virgin prayed fervently, imploring God not to allow them to freeze; and at once so great a warmth passed into her that she stretched out her hands to St.
49Joseph that he might warm himself by them.
50She took some food to renew her strength.
51The little ass, their guide, came up with them here and stood still.
52The actions of the little animal were truly astonishing.
53On straight roads, between mountains, for instance, where they could not go astray, she was sometimes behind, sometimes far ahead of them; but where the road branched, she was sure to make her appearance and run on the right way.
54Whenever they reached a spot at which they should halt, the little creature stood still.
55Joseph here spoke to Mary of the good lodgings that he expected to find in Bethlehem.
56He told her that he knew the good people of an inn at which, for a moderate sum, they could get a comfortable room.
57It was better, he said, to pay a little than to depend upon free quarters.
58He praised Bethlehem in order to console and encourage her.
59After that, I saw the Holy Family arrive at a large farmhouse, about two hours’ distance from the pine tree.
60The woman was not at home, and the man refused St.
61Joseph admittance, telling him that he might go on further.
62On they went until they came to a shepherd’s shed where they found the little ass, and where they too halted.
63There were some shepherds in it; but they soon vacated after showing themselves most friendly and supplying straw and faggots, or bundles of reeds for a fire.
64The shepherds then went to the house from which Mary and Joseph had been sent away.
65They mentioned having met them, and said: “What a beautiful, what an extraordinary woman! What an amiable, pious, benevolent man! What wonderful people those travelers are!” The man’s wife had now returned home, and she scolded at their having been sent away.
66I saw her going to the shepherd’s hut at which they had put up, but she was timid and dared not enter.
67This hut was on the north side of that mountain on whose southern declivity lay Samaria and Thebez.
68Toward the east of this region and on this side of the Jordan, Salem and Ainon are situated, and on the oppo-site side, Socoth.
69It was about twelve hours from Nazareth.
70The woman came again with her two children.
71She was quite friendly, and seemed to be very much touched by what she saw.
72The husband also came and begged pardon.
73After Mary and Joseph had refreshed themselves a little, he showed them to an inn about an hour further up the mountain.
74The host, however, excused himself to Joseph, pleading the numbers already there.
75But when the Blessed Virgin entered and begged for shelter, the wife of the innkeeper, as also the innkeeper himself, changed their bearing toward them.
76The man at once arranged a shelter for them under a neighboring shed, and took charge of the ass.
77The little she-ass was not with them.
78She was running around the fields; for when not needed, she did not make her appearance.
79This inn was a tolerably fine one, and consisted of several houses.
80Although situated on the north side of the mountain, it was surrounded by orchards, pleasure gardens, and balsam trees.
81Mary and Joseph remained overnight and the whole of the next day, for it was the Sabbath.
82On the Sabbath the hostess with her three children visited Mary, also the woman of that other house with her two children.
83Mary talked to the little ones and instructed them.
84They had little rolls of parchment from which they read.
85I, too, made bold to speak confidently to Mary.
86She told me how extremely well it was with her in her present condition.
87She felt no weight.
88But sometimes, she experienced a sensation of being so immensely large internally and as if she were hovering in her own person.
89She felt that she encompassed God and man, and that He whom she encompassed carried her.
90Joseph went out with the host to his fields.
91Both host and hostess had conceived great love for Mary; they sympathized with her condition.
92They pressed her to remain, and showed her a room which they would give her.
93But very early the next morning she started with Joseph on their journey.
94They went forward, a little more to the east, along the mountain and into a valley, increasing the distance between them and Samaria to which they seemed at first to be going.
95The temple upon Garizim was in sight.
96On the roof were numerous figures like lions or other animals, which shone with a white light in the sun.
97The road led down into a plain, or the field of Sichem.
98After a journey of about six leagues, they came to a solitary farmhouse where they were made welcome.
99The man was an overseer of fields and orchards belonging to a neighboring city.
100It was warmer here and vegetation more luxuriant than at any place they had been, for it was the sunny side of the mountain, and that makes a great difference in Palestine at this season.
101The house was not exactly in the valley, but on the southern declivity of the mountain which stretches from Samaria to the east.
102The occupants belonged to those shepherds with whose daughters later on, the servants remaining behind from the caravan of the Three Kings had married.
103In after years also Jesus often tarried here and taught.
104Before departing, Joseph blessed the children of the family.
105I saw him and Mary journeying over the plain beyond Sichem.
106The Blessed Virgin sometimes went on foot.
107They rested occasionally and refreshed themselves.
108They had with them little rolls and a cool, strengthening drink in nice little jugs, brown and shining like metal.
109The seat that Mary used on the ass was furnished with a pad on either side as a support for the limbs, which were thereby brought more into a sitting posture.
110The support was over the neck of the ass, and Mary sat sometimes to the right, sometimes to the left.
111Berries and other fruits were still hanging on the bushes and trees that were exposed to the sun, and these they gathered on the way.
112The first thing that Joseph always did on arriving at an inn, was to prepare a comfortable seat or couch for Mary; then he washed his feet, as did Mary also.
113Their ablutions were frequent.
114It was quite dark one evening when they reached a lonely inn.
115Joseph knocked and begged for shelter, but the owner would not open the door.
116Joseph explained to him his position, telling him that his wife could go no farther.
117But the man was inflexible; he would not interrupt his own rest.
118And when Joseph told him that he would pay him, he received for answer: “This is not an inn, I will not have that knocking.”
119 The door remained closed.
120Mary and Joseph went on for a short distance and found a shed.
121He struck a light, and prepared a couch for Mary, she herself assisting him.
122He brought the ass in, and found some straw and fodder for it.
123Here they rested a few hours.
124I saw them departing early the next morning while it was still dark.
125They may now have been distant from their last halting place about six hours, about six and twenty from Nazareth, and ten from Jerusalem.
126The last house stood on level ground, but the road from Gabatha to Jerusalem began again to grow steep.
127Up to this time Mary and Joseph travelled no great highroads, though they crossed several commercial routes which ran from the Jordan to Samaria and to the roads that lead from Syria down into Egypt.
128So far, the roads by which they came, with the exception of that single broad one, were very narrow and ran over the mountains.
129One had to be very cautious in walking, but the ass could tread its way securely.
130Now I saw the travelers arrive at a house whose owner was at first uncivil to Joseph.
131He threw the light on Mary’s face, and twitted Joseph on having so young a wife.
132But the man’s wife took them in, gave them shelter in an outhouse, and offered them some little rolls.
133When they left this place, they next sought lodging in a large farmhouse where also they were not received in a manner especially cordial.
134The innkeepers were young, and paid little heed to Mary and Joseph.
135They were not simple shepherds, but rich farmers, such as we have here, mixed up with the world, with trade, etc.
136I saw one old man going about the house with a walking stick.
137From here they had still seven hours’ journey to Bethlehem, but they did not take the direct route thither, because it was mountainous and at this season too difficult.
138They followed the little she-ass across the country between Jerusalem and the Jordan.
139I saw them arrive about noon at a large shepherd’s house, about two hours from John’s place of baptism on the Jordan.
140Jesus once passed a night there after His baptism.
141Near the house was another for the farm and sheep utensils, and in the yard was a spring from which the water was conducted through pipes to the bathtubs.
142There was a large public house here; and numbers of servants who took their meals at it were going and coming.
143The host received the travelers very kindly and he was very obliging.
144He insisted upon one of the servant’s washing Joseph’s feet at the spring.
145He also supplied him with fresh garments while he aired and brushed those he took off.
146A maidservant rendered the same services to Mary, for the mistress of the house was backward in making her appearance; she lived retired.
147She is the same that Jesus afterward healed of a thirty years’ sickness.
148He told her that her malady had come upon her as a punishment for her want of hospitality toward His relatives.
149But I know the reason of her nonappearance to Mary and Joseph.
150She was young and rather frivolous.
151She had caught a glance of the Blessed Virgin, had spoken a word to her, perhaps, (I do not now recall all the circumstances) and had conceived a feeling of jealousy on account of her beauty.
152It was for that reason that she kept herself secluded on this occasion.

154At their departure about noon, Mary and Joseph were accompanied part of the way by some of the people belonging to the inn.
155They proceeded westward toward Bethlehem, and arrived after a journey of about two hours at a little village consisting of a long row of houses with gardens and courts lying on both sides of a broad highroad.
156Joseph had connections here such as spring from the second marriage of a stepfather or stepmother.
157Their house was finely situated and very handsome.
158But Mary and Joseph did not enter.
159They passed through the place and went straight on toward Jerusalem for half an hour, when they came to a public house in which a crowd was gathered for a funeral.
160The frame partitions in the house had been removed from before the chim-ney and hearth.
161The fireplace was draped with black, and before it rested a coffin enveloped in the same somber hue.
162The male mourners wore long black robes with short, white ones over them and some had rough, black maniples on their arms.
163All were praying.
164In another apartment sat the women entirely enveloped in their large veils.
165There was in the yard a large fountain with several faucets.
166The proprietors of the house, who were taken up with the charge of the obsequies, left to the servants the duty of receiving Mary and Joseph.
167This was done, accordingly, and the customary services rendered the holy travelers.
168Tapestry, or mats, were let down from their rollers near the ceiling, and a curtained space arranged for them.
169After some time, I saw the people of the house in conversation with them.
170The white garments had been laid aside.
171I saw a great many beds rolled up against the walls.
172They could be entirely separated from one another by means of the mats let down from the ceilings.
173Early the following morning, Mary and Joseph again started off.
174The good wife of the house told them they might stay, because Mary appeared in hourly expectation of her delivery.
175But Mary said with lowered veil that she had yet six or eight and thirty hours.
176The woman was anxious to keep them, though not in her own house.
177I saw the husband, as Joseph and Mary were departing, talking to the former about his beasts.
178Joseph praised the ass very much, and told him that he had brought the other with him in case of necessity.
179When the people spoke of the dif-ficulty of getting lodgings in Bethlehem, Joseph replied that he had friends there and that Mary and he would certainly be well received.
180This made me feel so sorry.
181Joseph always spoke of this with so much confidence.
182I heard him again making the same remark to Mary on their way.
183It so happened on the last days of the journey, when they were nearing Bethlehem, that Mary sighed longingly for rest and refreshment.
184Joseph turned aside from the road for half an hour to a place where, upon a former occasion, he had discovered a beautiful fig tree laden with fruit.
185It had seats around it for weary wayfarers to rest upon.
186But when they reached it they found, to their great disappointment, that it was at that time quite destitute of fruit.
187In after years something connected with Jesus happened near that tree.
188It nevermore had fruit, though it continued green.
189Jesus cursed it, and it withered.
