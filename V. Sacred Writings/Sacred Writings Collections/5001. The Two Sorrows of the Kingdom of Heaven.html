Sacred Writing

"Every writings must be evaluated based on the truths they contain."

Chapter 1

1Why does one speak of sorrow in Heaven? [That] is not difficult.
2Elijah and Enoch prayed to the Lord to carry them in their bodies to Heaven.
3Due to the excellence, then, of their merit to God [while] on earth, they were carried to Paradise in their earthly bodies.
4Consequently, bright, pure, light, aerial souls are flying all around them in the shape of angels.
5They, that is, Elijah and Enoch, however, are in their heavy, sluggish, earthly bodies and they may not accompany [them].
6This is a sorrow and a great pain to them, [to be] unable to accompany the angels, so that they [themselves] are the two sorrows of the Kingdom of Heaven.


Chapter 2

1Elijah goes to sit under the Tree of Paradise [with] the Gospels in his hand to preach to the birds and the souls in Paradise.
2The birds then come to eat the fruit of the tree.
3Great berries are they, sweeter than any honey and more intoxicating than wine.
4They eat the fruit.
5After this Elijah opens his Gospels.
6At that they press their wings and feet against them, moving foot nor wing, until the preaching is finished.
7Teachings of Doomsday is what he preaches to them, that is, that which is brought down as tortures upon the souls of men on Doomsday,
8for instance, the four rivers around the mountain - that is, around the mountain of Zion - burning the souls.
9Long - that is, the thousand long years - is the danger for anyone who will be sinful.
10It is better for anyone who is well-deserving, even though it would be on that day only.
11Even if there were no suffering in store for the souls except on Doomsday, it would be meet that no-one should sleep thinking about it for as long as he should live.


Chapter 3

1In addition [to this] there is the coming of Christ with the nine orders of Heaven, with all mortal men who have been born and will be born up to the Last Judgment, and with the hosts of Hell.
2It is thus that he, Jesus Christ, will come to them, with his red cross on [his] back to avenge his crucifixion on the Devil by coming to deliver them from his mouth.
3The host and the crowd which will be there will be great.
4[It] is in front of that host then [that] everyone will show his deeds both good and bad.
5Everyone in turn [is] to reveal that which their eyes have seen, that which their lips and their tongues have said and their hands have made, and where their feet have been.
6Christ son of God and Heaven’s angels and [the] men of Earth and Hell listen to him until he finishes speaking.
7His accompanying devil [will be] reminding him of every evil thing he has done,
8for he used to be on his left hand side continually watching him.
9A guardian angel on his right hand side [will be] reminding him of the good he has done.


Chapter 4

1After all that has finished, Christ says: “Behold this [man], O judges! What is it which is heavier, this man’s goodness or badness?”
2“His goodness is heavier indeed.”
3“Let him go, then, with his goodness to the community he has chosen”, says Christ, “to the angels and archangels”.
4The angels come to fetch him [with] their hands outstretched towards him.
5“Welcome, come with me.”
6“He will come with me”, says the other.


Chapter 5

1“The goodness and badness of this man are equal.”
2“Half of this man [is] ours then” says the Devil.
3“The soul will not be divided,” says Christ “since my might is stronger [than yours] he will come with me”.


Chapter 6

1“The man’s evil is heavier, indeed.”
2“Let him go then with his evil to the community he has chosen.”
3The demons come to fetch him. “Alas for you,” they say, “Misery and suffering await you.”
4There will be sorrow to anyone: one man with his fist,
5the other man with his scourge, another [is] at his mouth with spikes and shouting at him.
6Miserable indeed [is he who] has earned the Devil’s welcome while he was in his body.


Chapter 7

1When, afterwards, they have finished the judging of Adam’s offspring,
2it is then that Christ will tell them to go with the Devil
3and his vulgar crowd, that is, those who have chosen [him] and placed [themselves] in his community in hell forever.
4They will rise after that and they will cry their unanimous cry as the Devil forces them into Hell.


Chapter 8

1There are three cries in the world, that is, [the first is] the cry of God’s people when they were forced by the Egyptians
2and the Pharaoh towards the Red Sea, to their death and destruction,
3and to give [into slavery] their sons and daughters forever, unless God would save them.
4And the cry of the men of Hell and the souls of Adam’s offspring who died before Christ’s crucifixion,
5that is, he brought them [out of Hell], to spite the Devil, into the environs of the Kingdom of Heaven.
6[This is] the cry, then, which the souls uttered on account of eagerness to escape from the Devil and the cry of the men of Hell in pursuit of them.
7The third cry, that is the cry of the sinners as they are cast into Hell to live forever in torment and punishment without [any] atonement at all.


Chapter 9

1Afterwards, the community which has chosen God will go towards the everlasting kingdom with Christ, son of the living God in the hosts of the archangels.
2The horrors, indeed, of that day - that is, Doomsday -, is what Elijah preaches under the Tree of Paradise, but this [is only] a selection of it.


Chapter 10

1As Elijah closes his book, the birds utter their cries and beat their wings against their sides, so that streams of blood come out of their sides for fear of Doomsday.
2If it is birds who do this, it would be fitting for us mortals to prepare [ourselves] for it [too].
3The Elijah and Enoch mentioned here, moreover, they will go to their martyrdom.
4For the Holy Spirit said: “Who is the man that shall live, and not [see] death?” that is, who experiences life and will not see death?
5They come to the Antichrist at the end of the world, and by him they are put to the sword, that is, that Antichrist [is] the Devil in the guise of a man. 6He comes into the world as if he were to bring faith.
7 [He will be] wearing a number on his forehead; this is the sign that will be on him. Anyone who will believe in him will be cut down by him, until Michael comes from heaven, so that it is he who will fight the Antichrist.
