From the Nativity of the Virgin Mary to the death of patriarch Saint Joseph

Chapter 1

The Arrival in Bethlehem

1The distance from the last public house to Bethlehem may have been three hours.
2Mary and Joseph went around by the north and approached the city on the west.
3A short distance outside the city, about a quarter of an hour’s walk brought them to a large building surrounded by courtyards and smaller houses.
4There were trees in front of it, and all sorts of people encamped in tents around it.
5This house was once the paternal home of Joseph, and ages before it had been the family mansion of David.
6It was at this period used as the custom house of the Roman taxes.
7Joseph still had in the city a brother, who was an innkeeper.
8He was not his own brother, but a stepbrother.
9Joseph did not go near him.
10Joseph had had five brothers, three own-brothers and two stepbrothers.
11Joseph was five and forty years old.
12He was thirty years and, I think, three months older than Mary.
13He was thin, had a fair complexion, prominent cheekbones tinged with red, a high, open forehead, and a brownish beard.
14 The little she-ass was not with them here.
15She had run away around the south side of the city, where it was somewhat level, a kind of valley.
16Joseph went straight into the custom house, for all newcomers had to present themselves there and obtain a ticket for entrance at the city gate.
17The city had properly no gate, but the entrance lay between two ruined walls that looked like the remains of a gate.
18Although Joseph was somewhat late in presenting himself for assessment, he was well received.
19 Mary remained in a small house in the courtyard among the women, who were very attentive to her, and offered her something to eat.
20These women cooked for the soldiers.
21The latter were Romans, as I could tell by the straps hanging around their hips.
22The weather was lovely, not at all cold, the sun lighting up the mountain between Jerusalem and Bethania.
23One can see it very well from here.
24Joseph went up to a large room in an upper story, where he was interrogated, who he was, etc., and his questioners examined long rolls of writing, numbers of which were hanging on the walls.
25They unrolled them and read to him his ancestry, also that of Mary.
26Joseph knew not before that through Joachim, Mary had descended in a straight line from David.
27The official asked him, “Where is thy wife?” 28For seven years the inhabitants of this part of the country were not regularly assessed, owing to various political troubles.
29I saw the numbers V and II, and that certainly makes seven.
30The tax collecting had already been going on for many months, but two payments were still to be made.
31The people had to remain almost three months.
32They had indeed paid something here and there during those seven years, but there had been no regular collection of taxes.
33Joseph did not pay anything on that first day, but his circumstances were inquired into.
34He told the official that he possessed no real estate, that he lived by his trade and the assistance of his wife’s parents.
35Mary also was summoned to appear before the clerk, but not upstairs.
36She was interrogated in a passage on the first floor, and nothing was read to her.
37There were numbers of clerks and functionaries in the house, scattered throughout the different rooms, and a great many Romans and soldiers were to be met in the upper stories.
38There were also Pharisees and Sadducees, priests and elders, and all sorts of clerks and officials of both Jewish and Roman extraction.
39There was no such payment of taxes going on in Jerusalem.
40But in many other places, in Magdalum on the Sea of Galilee, for instance, taxes were being received.
41The Galileans had to pay there, and the people from Sidon, too, partly on account of their commercial intercourse, I think.
42Only those that had no establishments, that possessed no estates, had to report at their birthplace.
43 The receipts for the next three months were to be divided into three parts.
44The Emperor Augustus, Herod, and another king who dwelt in the neighborhood of Egypt, had a share in them.
45The king near Egypt, having gained some advantage in war, had a claim upon a certain district far up the country; consequently, they had to give him something.
46The second payment had some reference to the building of the Temple; it was something like a payment on money advanced.
47The third was for the poor and for widows, who had received nothing for a long time.
48But it all went as such things do in our own day—little to the right man.
49Good reasons were easily found for its remaining in the hands of the great.
50Incessant writing and moving to and fro were kept up.
51 Joseph then went with Mary straight to Bethlehem on whose outskirts the houses stood scattered, and into the heart of the city.
52At the different streets they met, he left Mary and the ass standing while he went up and down in search of an inn.
53Mary often had to wait long before Joseph, anxious and troubled, returned.
54Nowhere did he find room; everywhere was he sent away.
55And now it began to grow dark.
56Joseph at last proposed going to the other side of the city, where they would surely find lodgings.
57They proceeded down a street, which was more of a country road than a regular street, for the houses stood scattered along the hills, and at the end of it reached a low, level space, or field.
58Here stood a very beautiful tree with a smooth trunk, its branches spreading out like a roof.
59Joseph led Mary and the beast under it, and there left them to go again in quest of an inn.
60He went from house to house, his friends, of whom he had spoken to Mary, unwilling to recognize him.
61Once during his quest, he returned to Mary, who was waiting under the tree.
62He wept, and she consoled him.
63He started afresh on his search.
64But whenever he brought forward the approaching delivery of his wife as a pressing reason for receiving hospitality, he was dismissed still more quickly.
65Meantime it had grown dark.
66Mary was standing under the tree, her ungirdled robe falling around her in full folds, her head covered with a white veil.
67The ass was nearby, its head turned toward the tree, at the foot of which Joseph had made a seat for Mary with the baggage.
68Crowds were hurrying to and fro in Bethlehem, and many of the passersby gazed curiously at Mary, as one naturally does on seeing a person standing a long time in the dark.
69I think also that some of them addressed her, and asked her who she was.
70Ah, they little dreamed that the Saviour was so near! Mary was so patient, so tranquil, so full of hope.
71Ah, she had indeed long to wait! At last she sat down, her hands crossed on her breast, her head lowered.
72After a long time, Joseph returned in great dejection.
73I saw that he was shedding tears and, because he had failed again to find an inn, he hesitated to approach.
74But suddenly he bethought him of a cave outside Bethlehem used as a storing place by the shepherds when they brought their cattle to the city.
75Joseph had often withdrawn thither to conceal himself from his brothers and to pray.
76It was very likely to be deserted at that season or, if any shepherds did come, it would be easy to make friends with them.
77He and Mary might there find shelter for awhile, and after a little rest he would go out again on his search.
78 And now they went around to the left, as if through the ruined walls, tombs, and ramparts of a country town.
79They mounted a rampart or hill, and then the road began again to descend.
80At last, they reached a hill before which stood trees, firs, pines, or cedars, and trees with small leaves like the box tree.
81In this hill was the cave or vault spoken of by Joseph.
82There were no houses around.
83One side of the cave was built up with rough masonry through which the open entrance of the shepherds led down into the valley.
84Joseph opened the light wicker door and, as they entered, the she-ass ran to meet them.
85She had left them near Joseph’s paternal house, and had run around the city to this cave.
86She frolicked around and leaped gaily about them, so that Mary said: “Behold! It is surely God’s will that we should be here.”
87But Joseph was worried and, in secret, a little ashamed, because he had so often alluded to the good reception they would meet in Bethlehem.
88There was a projection above the door under which he stood the ass and then proceeded to arrange a seat for Mary.
89It was quite dark, about eight o’clock when they reached this place.
90Joseph struck a light and went into the cave.
91The entrance was very narrow.
92The walls were stuffed with all kinds of coarse straw, like rushes, over which hung brown mats.
93Back in the vaulted part were some air holes in the roof, but here also everything was in disorder.
94Joseph cleared it out and prepared as much space in the back part as would afford room for a couch and seat for Mary, who had seated herself on a rug with her bundle for a support.
95The ass was then brought in, and Joseph fastened a lamp on the wall.
96While Mary was eating, he went out to the field in the direction of the Milk Cave, and laid a leathern bottle in the rivulet that it might fill.
97He went also to the city where he procured some little dishes, a bundle of other things, and I think, some fruit.
98It was, indeed, the Sabbath but, on account of the numerous strangers in the city and their need of various necessaries, provisions and utensils were exposed for sale on tables placed at the street corners.
99The price was paid down on the spot.
100I think servants or pagan slaves guarded the tables, but I cannot remember for certain.
101When Joseph returned, he brought with him a small bundle of slender sticks beautifully bound up with reeds, and a box with a handle in which were glowing coals.
102These he poured out at the entrance of the cave to make a fire.
103He next brought the water bottle, which he had filled at the rivulet, and prepared some food.
104It consisted of a stew, made of yellow corn, some kind of large plant that contained a great many seeds, and a little bread.
105After they had eaten and Mary had lain down to rest upon her rush couch over which was spread a cover, Joseph began to prepare his own resting place at the entrance of the cave.
106When this was done, he went again into the city.
107Previously to setting out, he had stopped up all the openings of the cave, in order to keep out the air.
108Then for the first time, I saw the Blessed Virgin on her knees in prayer, after which she lay down upon the carpet on her side, her head resting on her arm, her bundle serving for a pillow.
109This cave lay at the extremity of the mountain ridge of Bethlehem.
110A clump of beautiful trees stood in front of the entrance, and thence could be descried some of the towers and roofs of the city.
111Over the entrance, which was closed by a door made of wickerwork, was a shed.
112From the door, a moderately wide passage led into the cave, an irregularly formed vault, half-round, half-triangular.
113On one side of the passage was a recess rather lower than the general surface, and this Joseph had enclosed by curtains for his own sleeping place.
114The rest of the passage, from the recess to the entrance, he cut off by hangings, and there had a kind of storeroom.
115 The passage was not so lofty as the cave itself, which was vaulted by nature.
116The inner walls of the cave, where they were formed entirely by nature, though not perfectly even, yet were pleasing and clean; indeed to my eye, there was something about them quite charming.
117They pleased me more than did those parts upon which some attempts had been made at masonry, for these latter were coarse and rough.
118The foundation of the right side of the entrance appeared for some distance to have been hewn out of the rock; only the upper part seemed to have been made by the hand of man.
119There were also some holes in this passage.
120In the middle of the vaulted roof was an opening and, I think, three others cut obliquely halfway up the same.
121These oblique openings presented a smoother appearance than the topmost one; they looked like the handiwork of man.
122The floor of the cave was deeper than that of the entrance, and was on three sides surrounded by a stone seat somewhat raised, broad in some places, in others narrow.
123At one of the broad parts, the ass took its stand.
124It had no trough, but a large leathern bag was placed before it or hung in the corner.
125Behind was a small side cave just large enough to allow the animal to stand upright.
126There the fodder was stored.
127A gutter ran along by this corner, and I saw Joseph cleaning the cave out every day.
128Where Mary reposed before the birth of the Child and where I beheld her elevated above the ground at the moment of her delivery, there was a similar seat of stone.
129The spot in which the Crib stood was a deep recess, or side vault.
130Near it was a second entrance into the cave, which was in the ridge of a hill that ran toward the city.
131In the rear, the hill sank into a very charming valley planted with rows of trees.
132This valley led to the Suckling Cave of Abraham, situated in a projection of the opposite hill.
133The valley may have been one-eighth of an hour in width, and through it flowed that little rivulet from which Joseph had procured the water.
134Besides the real Crib Cave, there were in the same hill, but lying somewhat deeper, two other caves, in one of which the Blessed Virgin often remained hidden.
135When in after years St.
136Paula laid the first foundation of her convent at Bethlehem, I saw a small, lightly-built chapel erected in the valley and on the east side of the cave.
137It was so constructed as to be contiguous to the rear of the Crib Cave and directly back of the spot upon which Jesus was born.
138This little chapel of wood and wicker walls was hung inside with tapestry.
139Four rows of cells opened into it, which were built as lightly as the shepherd’s cots generally are in Palestine.
140In every row were separate cells, each surrounded by its own little garden, and all connected by covered passages leading to the chapel.
141Here Paula and her daughter gathered around them their first companions.
142In the chapel and free from the wall, stood an altar with its little tabernacle.
143Behind it hung a red and white silk curtain, which concealed the facsimile of the Crib Cave that St.
144Paula had caused to be made.
145It was separated from the real cave, from the exact spot upon which Jesus was born, only by the rocky wall.
146This crib was made of white stone, and was a faithful imitation of that of Jesus.
147The manger also was represented, and even the hay hanging through its sides.
148The infant in it was likewise of white stone, and closely swathed in a blue veil.
149The figure was hollow and not very heavy.
150I saw St.
151Paula often taking it up into her arms while she prayed.
152Upon the wall over this crib, hung a banner upon which was represented the ass with its head turned toward the crib.
153It was embroidered in colors, and the hair made of thread, so natural that it looked like real hair.
154Above the crib was a hole in which a star was fastened.
155I saw that the Child Jesus often appeared here to St.
156Paula and her daughter.
157In front of the curtain and right and left of the altar were hanging lamps.


Chapter 2

Birth of the Child Jesus

1I saw Joseph on the following day arranging a seat and couch for Mary in the so-called Suckling Cave of Abraham, which was also the sepulcher of Mar aha, his nurse.
2It was more spacious than the cave of the Crib.
3Mary remained there some hours, while Joseph was making the latter more habitable.
4He brought also from the city many different little vessels and some dried fruits.
5Mary told him that the birth hour of the Child would arrive on the coming night.
6It was then nine months since her conception by the Holy Ghost.
7She begged him to do all in his power that they might receive as honorably as possible this Child promised by God, this Child supernaturally conceived; and she invited him to unite with her in prayer for those hard-hearted people who would afford Him no place of shelter.
8Joseph proposed to bring some pious women whom he knew in Bethlehem to her assistance; but Mary would not allow it, she declared that she had no need of anyone.
9It was five o’clock in the evening when Joseph brought Mary back again to the Crib Cave.
10He hung up several more lamps, and made a place under the shed before the door for the little she-ass, which came joyfully hurrying from the fields to meet them.
11When Mary told Joseph that her time was drawing near and that he should now betake himself to prayer, he left her and turned toward his sleeping place to do her bidding.
12Before entering his little recess, he looked back once toward that part of the cave where Mary knelt upon her couch in prayer, her back to him, her face toward the east.
13He saw the cave filled with the light that streamed from Mary, for she was entirely enveloped as if by flames.
14It was as if he were, like Moses, looking into the burning bush.
15He sank prostrate to the ground in prayer, and looked not back again.
16The glory around Mary became brighter and brighter, the lamps that Joseph had lit were no longer to be seen.
17Mary knelt, her flowing white robe spread out before her.
18At the twelfth hour, her prayer became ecstatic, and I saw her raised so far above the ground that one could see it beneath her.
19Her hands were crossed upon her breast, and the light around her grew even more resplendent.
20I no longer saw the roof of the cave.
21Above Mary stretched a pathway of light up to Heaven, in which pathway it seemed as if one light came forth from another, as if one figure dissolved into another, and from these different spheres of light other heavenly figures issued.
22Mary continued in prayer, her eyes bent low upon the ground.
23At that moment she gave birth to the Infant Jesus.
24I saw Him like a tiny, shining Child, lying on the rug at her knees, and brighter far than all the other brilliancy.
25He seemed to grow before my eyes.
26But dazzled by the glittering and flashing of light, I know not whether I really saw that, or how I saw it.
27Even inanimate nature seemed stirred.
28The stones of the rocky floor and the walls of the cave were glimmering and sparkling, as if instinct with life.
29Mary’s ecstasy lasted some moments longer.
30Then I saw her spread a cover over the Child, but she did not yet take It up, nor even touch It.
31After a long time, I saw the Child stirring and heard It crying, and then only did Mary seem to recover full consciousness.
32She lifted the Child, along with the cover that she had thrown over It, to her breast and sat veiled, herself and Child quite enveloped.
33I think she was suckling It.
34I saw angels around her in human form prostrate on their faces.
35It may, perhaps, have been an hour after the birth when Mary called St.
36Joseph, who still lay prostrate in prayer.
37When he approached, he fell on his knees, his face to the ground, in a transport of joy, devotion, and humility.
38Mary again urged him to look upon the Sacred Gift from Heaven, and then did Joseph take the Child into his arms.
39And now the Blessed Virgin swathed the Child in red and over that in a white veil up as far as under the little arms, and the upper part of the body from the armpits to the head, she wrapped up in another piece of linen.
40She had only four swaddling cloths with her.
41She laid the Child in the Crib, which had been filled with rushes and fine moss over which was spread a cover that hung down at the sides.
42The Crib stood over the stone trough, and at this spot the ground stretched straight and level as far as the passage, where it made a broader flexure toward the south.
43The floor of this part of the cave lay somewhat deeper than where the Child was born, and down to it steps had been formed in the earth.
44When Mary laid the Child in the Crib, both she and Joseph stood by It in tears, singing the praises of God.
45The seat and the couch of the Blessed Virgin were near the Crib.
46I saw her on the first day sitting upright and also resting on her side, though I noticed in her no special signs of weakness or sickness.
47Both before and after the birth, she was robed in white.
48When visitors came, she generally sat near the Crib more closely veiled.
49On the night of the Birth there gushed forth a beautiful spring in the other cave that lay to the right.
50The water ran out, and the next day Joseph dug a course for it and formed a spring.
51In those visions to which the event itself, and not the feast of the Church, gave rise, I saw, indeed, no such sparkling joy in nature as I sometimes see at holy Christmastide.
52Then the joy has an interior signification.
53But yet, I saw extraordinary gladness, and in many places, even in the most distant regions of the world, something marvelous on that midnight.
54By it the good were filled with joyful longings, and the bad with dread.
55I saw also many of the lower animals joyfully agitated.
56I saw fountains gushing forth and swelling, flowers springing up in many places, trees and plants budding with new life, and all sending forth their fragrance.
57In Bethlehem it was misty, and the sky above shone with a murky, reddish glare.
58But over the valley of the shepherds, around the Crib, and in the vale of the Suckling Cave floated bright clouds of refreshing dew.
59I saw the herds of the three oldest shepherds near the hill under sheds; but those further on near the shepherds’ tower, were partly in the open air.
60The three eldest shepherds, roused by the wonders of the night, I saw standing together before their huts, gazing around and pointing out the magnificent light that shone over the Crib.
61The shepherds at the distant tower were also in full movement.
62They had climbed up the tower and were looking toward the Crib over which they, too, saw the light.
63I saw something like a cloud of glory descend upon the three shepherds.
64I saw in it figures moving to and fro, and heard the approach of sweet, clear voices singing softly.
65At first, the shepherds were frightened.
66Soon there stood before them five or seven lovely, radiant figures holding in their hands a long strip like a scroll upon which were written words in letters a hand in length.
67The angels were singing, the angels appeared also to the shepherds on the tower and where else, I do not now recall.
68I did not see them hurrying off at once to the cave.
69The first three were indeed an hour and a half distant from it, and those on the tower as far again.
70But I saw that they began at once to reflect upon what gifts they should take to the newborn Saviour, and to get them together as quickly as possible.
71The three shepherds went to the Crib early next morning.
72I saw that Anne at Nazareth, Elizabeth in Juttah, Noemi, Anna, and Simeon in the Temple—all had on this night visions from which they learned the birth of the Saviour.
73The child John was unspeakably joyous.
74But only Anne knew where the newborn Child was; the others, and even Elizabeth, knew indeed of Mary and saw her in vision, but they knew nothing of Bethlehem.
75I saw something very wonderful taking place in the Temple.
76The writings of the Sadducees were more than once hurled by an invisible force from the places in which they were kept, which circumstance gave rise to unaccountable dread.
77The fact was ascribed to sorcery, and large sums of money were paid to hush the matter up.
78I saw that in Rome, across the river where numbers of Jews dwelt, a well of oil gushed forth spon-taneously, to the wonder of all the witnesses.
79And when Jesus was born, a magnificent statue of the god Jupiter fell with violence from its place.
80All were struck with fear.
81Sacrifices were offered and another idol, I think Venus, was interrogated as to the cause.
82The devil was forced to speak by its mouth, and he proclaimed that it had happened because a virgin unmarried had conceived and brought forth a son.
83He told them also of the miracle of the oil well.
84Where this took place now stands a church in honor of the Mother of God.
85I saw that the pagan priests were deeply perplexed at the whole affair.
86They searched their writings, and discovered the following history.
87About seventy years previously, this idol (Jupiter) had been greatly venerated.
88It was magnificently ornamented with gold and precious stones, grand ceremonies were held in its honor, and numer-ous sacrifices offered to it.
89But there was in Rome at that time an extraordinarily pious woman who lived on her own means.
90I know not for certain whether she was a Jewess or not; but she had visions, uttered prophecies, and informed many persons as to the cause of their sterility.
91This woman had thrown out words to this effect that they should not honor the idol at so great a cost, for that they would one day behold it burst asunder in their midst.
92This speech proved so offensive that she was imprisoned and tormented until by her prayers she obtained from God the information as to when that misfortune would happen.
93The pagan priests demanded what had been revealed to her, and when at last she replied: “The idol will be shattered when an Immaculate Virgin shall bring forth a son,” they hooted at her, and released her as a fool.
94And now the people recalled the fact and declared that the woman had spoken truly.
95I saw also that the Roman consuls, of whom one was named Lentulus and who was a friend of St.
96Peter and an ancestor of the martyr-priest Moses, made notes of this occurrence, as well as that of the bursting forth of the oil well.
97On this night, I saw the Emperor Augustus at the Capitol where he had an apparition of a rainbow upon which sat the Virgin and Child.
98From the oracle that he caused to be interrogated upon what he had seen, he received the answer: “A Child is born, and before Him we must all flee!” The emperor at once erected an altar and offered sacrifice to the Son of the Virgin, as to the “Firstborn of God.”
99I had also a vision of Egypt far beyond Matarea, Heliopolis, and Memphis.
100There was in that region a large idol that used to give answers to all kinds of questions.
101Suddenly it became mute.
102The king ordered immense sacrifices to be offered throughout his whole dominions.
103Then was the devil, upon the command of God, forced to say: “I have become silent, I must give place to another.
104The Son of the Virgin is born, and a temple will be here erected to His honor.”
105 Upon hearing this, the king wanted to raise a temple to the newborn Child next to that of the god, but I do not clearly recall the story.
106I know, however, that the idol was put aside and that a temple was erected to the Virgin and Child whom it had proclaimed, and who were afterward honored with pagan rites.
107I beheld a great wonder in the country of the Three Kings.
108There was a tower on a mountain to which the Kings retired in turn with a retinue of priests, in order to observe the stars.
109What they saw they committed to writing and communicated to one another.
110On this night there were two of them there, Mensor and Seir.
111The third, who dwelt toward the east side of the Caspian Sea, was called Theokeno.
112He was not present.
113There was a certain constellation at which they always gazed, and whose variations they noted.
114In it they saw visions and pictures.
115Upon this night also, they had several visions of various kinds.
116It was not in one star alone that they saw those visions, but in several that formed a figure, and there seemed to be a movement in them.
117They saw the vision of the moon over which arose a beautiful rainbow-colored arch on which was seated a Vir-gin.
118The left limb was drawn up in a sitting posture, the right hung a little lower and rested on the moon.
119To the left of the Virgin and rising above the arch, was a grapevine, and on her right a sheaf of wheat.
120In front of the Virgin was a chalice like that used at the Last Supper.
121It appeared to issue, but with greater clearness and brightness, from the brilliancy that emanated from her.
122Out of the chalice arose a Child, and over the Child shone a bright disk like an empty ostensorium.
123It was surrounded by radiating beams.
124It reminded me of the Blessed Sacrament.
125On the Virgin’s right was an octangular church with a golden door and two small side-doors.
126With the right hand, the Virgin put the Child and the host into the church which, meanwhile, grew larger and larger, and in which I saw the Most Holy Trinity.
127Above the church arose a tower.
128Theokeno, the third king, had similar visions in his own home.
129Over the head of the Virgin sitting on the arch shone a star, which suddenly shot from its place and skimmed along the heavens before the Kings.
130It was for them a voice announcing as never before that the Child, so long awaited by them and by their ancestors, was at last born in Judea, and that they were to follow that star.
131For some nights immediately preceding that blessed one, they had from their tower seen all kinds of visions in the heavens, kings journeying to the Child and offering their homage to It.
132So now they hurriedly gathered together their treasures and with gifts and presents began the journey, for they did not want to be the last.
133I saw all three after a few days meeting on the way.


Chapter 3

Adoration of the Shepherds. Devout Visits to the Crib

1In the early dawn after the birth of Jesus, the three oldest of the shepherds came to the Crib Cave with the gifts they had gathered together.
2These consisted of little animals bearing some resemblance to deer.
3They were very lightly built and nimble, had long necks and clear, beautiful eyes.
4They followed or ran along beside the shepherds who led them with fine, guiding cords.
5The shepherds carried also large, live birds under their arms, and dead ones slung over their shoulders.
6They told Joseph at the entrance of the cave what the angel had announced to them, and that they had come to do homage to the Child of Promise and to offer Him gifts.
7Joseph accepted their presents and allowed them to lead the animals into the space that formed a kind of cellar near the side entrance of the cave.
8Then he conducted them to the Blessed Virgin, who was sitting on the ground near the Crib, a rug under her, the Infant Jesus on her lap.
9The shepherds, their staves resting on their arms, fell on their knees and wept with joy.
10They knelt long, tasting great interior sweetness, and then intoned the angelic canticle of praise, and a Psalm that I have forgotten.
11When they were about to take leave, Mary placed the Child in their arms.
12Some of the other shepherds came in the evening, accompanied by women and children, and bringing gifts.
13They sang most sweetly before the Crib the some Psalms, and short refrains of which I remember the words: “O Child, blooming as a rose art Thou! As a herald Thou comest forth!”
14They brought gifts of birds, eggs, honey, woven stuffs of various colors, bundles of raw silk, and ears of corn, also several bundles of a corn with heavy grains growing on a stalk with large leaves like those of rushes.


Chapter 4

Cave of the Nativity, Bethlehem

1(The site of the Nativity is attested to by an unbroken tradition dating back to the 2nd Century.
2This plan is a suggested reconstruction of the original arrangement based on the description in the “ and the present form of the caves (shaded areas).
3During the centuries there have been many additions and enlargements.
4Sister Emmerich stated that new grottoes were cut in the rock even during the lifetime of Our Lord.
5The basilica, erected by Constantine over the cave in the 4th Century, and still in use, is one of the oldest Christian churches in the world).
6The three oldest shepherds came back in turn and helped Joseph to make the Crib Cave and its surroundings more comfortable.
7I saw also several pious women with the Blessed Virgin, performing some services for her.
8They were Essenians, and lived in the valley, not far from the Crib Cave, in little rocky cells adjoining one another.
9They owned little gardens near their cells, and they taught the children of their community.
10St. Joseph had invited them to come, for he was acquainted with them even in early youth.
11When he was hiding in the Crib Cave, from his brethren, he visited these pious women who dwelt in the side of the rock.
12They now came in turn to the Blessed Virgin, bringing little necessaries and bundles of wood.
13They cooked and washed for the Holy Family.
14Some days after the birth of Jesus, I saw a touching scene in the Crib Cave.
15Joseph and Mary were standing by the Crib and gazing with emotion upon the Infant Jesus, when suddenly the ass fell upon its knees and lowered its head to the ground.
16Mary and Joseph shed tears.
17I saw Mary at another time standing by the Crib.
18As she gazed upon the Child, the deep conviction stole upon her that It had come upon earth to suffer.
19That reminded me of a vision I had had at an earlier period in which I had been shown how Jesus, while still in His Mother’s womb and from the moment of His birth, had suffered.
20I saw under the heart of Mary a glory and in it a bright shining Child.
21As I gazed upon It, it seemed as if Mary were hovering over It and surrounding It.
22I beheld the Child growing and all the torments of the Crucifixion inflicted upon It.
23It was a sad, a fearful sight! I wept and sobbed aloud.
24I saw other forms around It beating and pushing, scourging and crowning It.
25Then they laid the Cross upon It, next nailed It to the same, and pierced It in the side.
26I saw the whole Passion of Christ in the Child.
27It was a frightful sight! As the Child hung on the Cross, It said to me: “All this did I suffer from My conception until My thirty-fourth year, when My Passion was outwardly consummated.”
28(The Lord died when He was thirty-three years and three months old.)
29“Go and announce it to men!” But how can I announce it to men?
30I saw Jesus also as the newborn Child, and I saw how many of the children that went to the Crib ill treated the Infant Jesus.
31The Mother of God was not there to protect the Child, and the children went with all kinds of switches and rods, and struck It in the face until the Blood flowed.
32The Child meekly extended Its little hands before Its face, in order to ward off the blows.
33The smallest children were they that struck the most maliciously.
34The parents of some even twisted and wrapped the rods for them.
35They brought thorns, nettles, whips, little rods of all kinds, each having its own signification.
36One came with a very slender rod, like a reed.
37But when it was about to strike the Child, the rod snapped, and fell back upon itself.
38I knew several of the children.
39Some went about boasting in their fine clothes, but I stripped them, and whipped some of them well.
40While Mary was still standing by the Crib in deep meditation, some shepherds drew near with their wives, in all about five persons.
41To give them room to approach the Crib, the Blessed Virgin withdrew a little to the spot upon which she had given birth to the Child.
42The people did not actually adore, but they gazed down upon the Child deeply moved, and before leaving they bowed low over It as if kissing It.
43It was day.
44Mary sat in her usual place with the Infant Jesus on her lap.
45He was swathed, the hands and face alone free, Mary had something like a piece of linen in her hands with which she was busied.
46Joseph was at the fireplace near the entrance of the cave, and appeared to be making a shelf to hold some vessels.
47I was standing next the ass.
48And now came in three aged female Essenians, who were cordially welcomed, though Mary did not rise.
49They brought quite a number of presents: small fruits, birds with red, awl-shaped beaks as large as ducks, which they carried by the wings, oval rolls about an inch in thickness, some linen, and other stuff.
50All were received with rare humility and gratitude.
51The women were very silent and recollected.
52Deeply moved, they gazed down upon the Child, but they did not touch It.
53When they withdrew, it was without farewells or ceremony.
54Meanwhile, I was taking a good look at the ass.
55It had a very broad back, and I thought to myself: “You good beast! You have carried a great burden!”1 and I wanted to feel it, to see if it were real.
56I ran my hand over its hair, and it felt as smooth as silk.
57Now came two married women with three little girls about eight years old.
58They appeared to be strangers and people of distinction, who had come in obedience to a call more miraculous than that received by any previous visitor.
59Joseph welcomed them very humbly.
60They brought presents less in size than the others, but of greater value: grain in a bowl, small fruits, and a cluster of thick, triangular, gold leaves on which was a stamp like a seal.
61I thought: “Strange! That looks like the representation of the eye of God! But no! How can I compare the eye of God with red earth!” Mary arose and placed the Child in the ladies’ arms.
62Both held Him a little while, praying silently with uplifted heart, and then kissed Him.
63The three little girls were silent and deeply impressed.
64Joseph and Mary conversed with their visitors and when they left, Joseph accompanied them part of the way.
65Ah! Could we, like these women, behold the beauty, the purity, the innocent wisdom of Mary! She knew all things! But in her humility, she appears unconscious of her gifts.
66Like a child, she casts down her eyes; and when she raises them, her glance, like a flash of lightning, like the truth, like a ray of unsullied light, pierces one through and through.
67That is because she is perfectly pure, perfectly innocent, full of the Holy Ghost, and without any reflection on self.
68No one can resist her glance.
69These people appeared to have come at least some miles and that secretly, for they avoided being seen in the city.
70Joseph behaved with great humility during such visits, retiring and looking on from some distant corner.
71I saw also Anne’s maid and an old man servant coming from Nazareth to the Crib.
72The maid was a widow and related to the Holy Family.
73She brought all sorts of necessaries from Anne to Mary, with whom she took up her abode.
74The old man shed tears of joy, and returned with news to Anne.
75The day after, I saw the Blessed Virgin and the Infant Jesus leave the Crib Cave with the maid for some hours.
76Stepping from the door of the cave, Mary turned toward the shed on the right, went some steps forward, and concealed herself in that side cave in which, at the birth of Jesus, a spring had welled up.
77She remained there four hours, because some men, spies of Herod, had come from Bethlehem, in consequence of the rumor set afloat by the words of the shepherds, that a miracle had there taken place in connection with a child.
78These men met St.
79Joseph in front of the Crib Cave.
80After exchanging a few words with him, they left him with a contemptuous smile at his humility and simplicity.
81The Crib Cave was retired, and very pleasantly situated.
82No one from Bethlehem went there, only the shepherds whose duties called them thither.
83No one in Bethlehem took any interest in what was going on outside for, in consequence of the influx of strangers, the city was all alive, and much buying and selling going on.
84Cattle was being bought and slaughtered, for many people paid their taxes in cattle.
85There were numbers of pagans in the city in the capacity of servants.
86The wonderful apparition of the angels was soon noised among the dwellers of the mountain valleys far and near, and with it the birth of the Child in the cave.
87The innkeepers from whom the Holy Family on their journey had received hospitality now came, one after another, to do homage to Him whom unknown they had entertained.
88I saw that hospitable keeper of the last inn, first sending presents by a servant, and then coming himself to honor the Child.
89I saw also the good wife of that man who had been so cross to Joseph, and other shepherds and good people coming to the Crib.
90They were very much affected by what they saw.
91All were in holiday attire, and were going up to Bethlehem for the Sabbath.
92The good wife might have gone to Jerusalem which was nearer, but she preferred coming here to Bethlehem.
93A relative of Joseph, and father of that Jonadab who, at the Crucifixion of Jesus presented a strip of linen to Him, had also come to the Crib Cave on his way to Bethlehem for the Sabbath.
94Joseph was very kind to him.
95This relative had heard from people of his place of Joseph’s wonderful situation; he came therefore to bring him gifts and to visit the Infant Jesus and Mary.
96But Joseph would not accept anything, although he pawned the little she-ass to this relative with the understanding that she might be redeemed for the same amount of money received.
97After that, Mary, Joseph, the maid, and two of the shepherds who were standing in front of the entrance, celebrated the Sabbath in the Crib Cave.
98A lamp with seven wicks was lighted, and upon a small table covered with white and red, lay the prayer rolls.
99The numerous eatables presented by the shepherds were either given to the poor or handed around for the entertainment of others.
100The birds were hung on a spit before the fire, turned from time to time, and sprinkled with the flour of a reed-like plant which was very plentiful around the area of Bethlehem and Hebron.
101From its grain a shining, white jelly was prepared and cakes baked.
102I saw under the fireplace very hot and clean holes in which birds could be roasted.
103After the Sabbath, the Essenian women got a meal ready under the arbors which Joseph, with the help of the shepherds, had put up at the entrance of the cave.
104Joseph went into the city to engage priests for the circumcision of the Child.
105The cave was cleared and put in order.
106The partition that Joseph had put up in the passage was removed, and the ground spread with carpets, for in this passage near the Crib Cave, the place for the ceremony was prepared.


Chapter 5

The Circumcision

1Joseph returned from Bethlehem with five priests and a woman whose services were necessary on such occasions.
2They brought with them the circumcision stool and an octangular slab with all that was needed for the ceremony.
3All this was placed in order in the passage.
4The stool was hollow and formed a chest, which could be taken apart, thus affording a kind of low seat with a support on the side.
5It was covered with red.
6The circumcision stone was, perhaps, over two feet in diameter.
7In the center was a metal plate under which, in a hollow of the stone, were all kinds of little boxes containing fluids.
8These boxes were in separate compartments, and at one side lay the circumcision knife.
9The stone was laid upon the little stool which, covered with a cloth, always stood on the spot upon which Jesus was born, and the circumcision stool was placed next to it.
10That evening a repast was spread under the arbor at the entrance to the cave.
11A crowd of poor people had followed the priests, as is usual on such occasions, and during the meal they were continually receiving something both from the priests and from Joseph.
12The priests went to Mary and the Child, spoke with the mother, and took the Child in their arms.
13They also spoke to Joseph about the name the Child was to receive.
14They prayed and sang the greater part of the night, and circumcised the Child at daybreak.
15Mary was very much troubled, very anxious about It.
16After the ceremony, the Infant Jesus was swathed in red and white as far as under the little arms, which also were bound and the head wrapped in a cloth.
17The Child was again laid on the octangular stone, and prayers recited over It.
18If I remember rightly, the angel had already told Joseph that the Child should be called Jesus, and I have a faint recollection that one of the priests did not at first approve the name, consequently, they still continued in prayer.
19Then I saw a radiant angel stand-ing in front of the priest and holding before him a tablet like that above the Cross, upon which was inscribed the name of Jesus.
20I saw the priest writing the name upon a scrap of parchment.
21I know not whether he or any of the others saw the angel, but deeply moved, he wrote the name under divine inspiration.
22After that, Joseph received the Child back and handed It to the Blessed Virgin who, with two other women, was standing back in the Crib Cave.
23Mary took the weeping Child into her arms and quieted It.
24Some shepherds were standing at the entrance of the cave.
25Lamps were burning, and the dawn was breaking.
26There was some more praying and singing and, before the priests departed, they took a little breakfast.
27I saw that all present at the circumcision were good people.
28The priests were enlightened and later attained salvation.
29Alms were distributed the whole morning to many poor people who presented themselves.
30Afterward followed a crowd of beggars, filthy, black creatures, very repulsive to me.
31They carried bundles and, coming up from the valley of the shepherds, passed the Crib as if going to Jerusalem for the celebration of a feast.
32They were very boisterous, cursing and scolding horribly, because they did not receive by way of alms, as much as they wanted.
33I do not know exactly what was the matter with them.
34During the ceremony of circumcision, the ass was tied further back than usual; at other times, it stood in the Crib Cave.
35During the day, I saw the nurse again with Mary attending to the Child.
36That night, the Child was very restless from pain.
37It cried, and Mary and Joseph tried to soothe It by carrying It up and down the cave.
38While reflecting upon the mystery of the circumcision, I had a vision.
39I saw two angels with little tablets in their hands, standing under a palm tree.
40Upon one tablet were pictured various instruments of martyrdom, of which I remember one, a pillar which stood in the middle.
41On it was a mortar, which had two rings.
42On the other tablet were letters denoting the seasons and years of the Church.
43On the palm tree and as if growing out of it, was kneeling a Virgin, her flowing mantle, or veil, for it was fastened over her head, floating around her.
44In her hands was a heart upon which I saw a tiny, shining Child.
45I saw an apparition of God the Father draw near to the palm tree, break off a heavy branch that formed a cross, and lay it on the Child.
46Then I saw the Child raised, as it were, on the cross, and the Virgin reaching the palm branch with the crucified Child on it to God the Father, the heart alone remaining in her hand.
47On the evening of the following day, I saw Elizabeth on an ass and accompanied by an old servant, coming from Juta to the cave.
48Joseph received her most cordially.
49The joy of Mary and Elizabeth was extremely great as they embraced each other.
50Elizabeth pressed the Child to her heart.
51She slept in Mary’s cave next the place in which Jesus was born.
52Before the sacred spot stood a stool upon which they often laid the Child.
53Mary told Elizabeth all that had happened to her, and when Elizabeth heard of their difficulty in getting a lodging on their arrival in Bethlehem, she wept heartily.
54Mary gave her all the details of the Infant Jesus’ birth.
55I remember hearing her say that she had been in ecstasy ten minutes at the time of the Annunciation, that it appeared to her as if her heart had grown double its size and that she was filled with unspeakable happiness.
56But at the Child’s birth she had experienced an intense longing.
57She felt while kneeling that she was upheld by angels, and as if her heart was broken asunder and one-half taken from her.
58She had also been ten minutes in ecstasy at the time of the birth.
59She had been conscious of an emptiness within her, a longing after something outside of herself.
60Suddenly a light shone before her, and the figure of the Child seemed to grow before her eyes.
61Then she saw It moving and heard It crying and, coming to herself, she raised It from the rug to her breast, for at first seeing It environed with glory, she had hesitated to take It up.
62Elizabeth said: “Thou hast not given birth in the same way as other mothers.
63The birth of John was sweet also, but it was not like that of thy Child.”
64Once I saw Elizabeth with Mary and the Child concealing themselves toward evening in the side cave.
65They remained there the whole night, for visitors from Bethlehem were approaching by whom they did not want to be seen.
66The Jewish women do not leave their children long without other nourishment than the breast; and so the Infant Jesus was fed in those first days on pap made of the sweet, light, nutritious pith of a certain rush-like plant.
67As in the Temple at Jerusalem, the holy Feast of the Machabees began at this time, it was also celebrated by Joseph in the Crib Cave.
68He fastened three lamps with seven little lights on the walls of the cave and, during a whole week, lighted them morning and evening.
69Once I saw in the cave one of the priests who had been present at the Child’s circumcision.
70He had a roll of writings from which he prayed with St.
71Joseph.
72It seemed to me that he wanted to find out whether Joseph kept that feast or not.
73I think, too, that he announced to him another, for a fast-day was near at hand.
74I saw the preparations for it in Jerusalem.
75Food was prepared the day before the feast, the fire was covered, servile work was put aside, the doors and windows were hung with tapestry.
76Anne often sent servants with gifts of provisions and utensils, all of which Mary soon distributed to the poor.
77Once Anne sent a beautiful little basket of fruit with large, newly-blown roses stuck in among it.
78The pink roses were paler than ours, almost flesh colored, and there were some yellow, and some white.
79Mary was very much pleased, and placed it beside her.
80And now came Anne herself, accompanied by her second husband and a servant.
81The Infant Jesus stretched out His little arms to her, and great was her joyful emotion.
82Mary gave her a full account of all as she had done to Elizabeth.
83They mingled their tears together, pausing at times to fondle the Infant Jesus.
84Anne had brought with her many things for Mary and the Child, coverlets, swathing-bands, etc.
85Although Mary had already received so many things from her, yet the Crib Cave was still quite poor in appearance, since whatever was at all unnecessary was given away at once.
86Mary told Anne that the Kings from the East were approaching with rich gifts, and that their coming would attract much attention.
87Anne, therefore, resolved to go and stay with her sister, who dwelt at some hours’ distance, and to return after the departure of the royal visitors.
88Then I saw Joseph set to work to clear out the Crib Cave as well as those in its vicinity, in order to prepare for the arrival of the Kings whom Mary in spirit had seen coming.
89He went also to Bethlehem to make the second payment of taxes and to look around for a dwelling, for he intended to settle in Bethlehem after Mary’s Purification.
