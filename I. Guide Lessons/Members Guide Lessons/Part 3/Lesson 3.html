NOT MY WILL BUT THY WILL BE DONE LORD

God’s will is shown in His Word. Understanding His wisdom helps us know who He is and what He wants from us.

No matter what we've learned, giving up our will to God is necessary for anyone who wants to truly follow and serve Him.

How to Walk Like Jesus...
In Luke 22:42, Jesus says, “Father, if you are willing, take this cup from me; yet not my will, but Yours be done.”

The idea of surrendering our will to God is a key message of the Bible.

Our human nature can be very deceptive and rebellious, making people think God doesn't care about what we say or do.

Pray for wisdom to understand that our disobedience is a serious issue.

Our emotions don't care if we are spiritually lost as long as we are in control of our decisions. But God should be the one making the decisions.

Adam and Eve chose their own way despite God's warnings, showing the rebellious nature of all humanity.

Remember, ignoring God's warnings leads to serious and unchangeable consequences.

Each child of God has a unique role in His Kingdom.

Our different gifts, talents, and personalities are meant to fulfill His perfect plan.

Living as Christians means we have responsibilities that we need to understand and follow for the rest of our lives.

True wisdom begins with respecting God, which means we must rely on His strength and attitude for success.

Those who try to mix their will with God’s will or let denial lead them into subtle rebellion will find their efforts fail.

Even if they work hard, they end up feeling empty and dissatisfied.

Why? Because God does not accept or bless offerings made on our terms.

Until we truly give up control and yield our will to Him, we haven’t really surrendered to God.
