Proverbs 3:4

NLT (New Living Translation)

4 "Then you will find favor with both God and people,and you will earn a good reputation."


GUIDE REFLECTION:


The Love of God and Dependability—Pathways to Favor and Good Success

The love of God and dependability are powerful forces in shaping a life that is blessed, respected, and impactful. In both God’s eyes and human perception, these qualities often bring favor and good success. In the context of wisdom literature, the point is not that favor and success are guaranteed in every situation. Instead, it highlights a pattern: those who align with God’s will tend to experience better outcomes than those who defy it.

The Hebrew word for "favor" is rich in meaning, originating from a verb that implies graciousness or kindness. It encompasses "grace," "kindheartedness," and "charm," and can even suggest popularity or approval. This favor, however, is deeper than mere admiration or popularity. True favor results from an alignment with God’s character, extending beyond superficial approval to a genuine sense of goodwill and respect.

"Good success" carries a similar weight. It implies a reputation for competence, wisdom, and effectiveness—a person who is not only skilled but is also wise and principled in their actions. Jesus, even at a young age, exemplified this balance. As Luke 2:40 records, Jesus “grew and became strong, filled with wisdom. And the favor of God was upon him.” His life mirrored a model of divine and human respect. Luke 2:52 reinforces this, saying, “And Jesus increased in wisdom and in stature and in favor with God and man.” Jesus’ life became a clear example that favor and success, in both divine and human sight, follow a life rooted in God’s wisdom and will.

This principle also finds expression in Paul’s instruction for overseers in the church. One of the essential qualities of a church leader, according to 1 Timothy 3:7, is to be “well thought of by outsiders.” Here, Paul emphasizes that leaders must not only have a good reputation within the faith community but also be respected by those outside it. This respect underscores the importance of living a blameless, dependable life that testifies to God’s love in a way that others find admirable.

For churches and believers today, the qualities of loving God and being dependable hold significant value. When these qualities are present, believers become people of influence in their communities. Love for God shapes a believer’s motives, actions, and decisions, while dependability builds trust, stability, and a good reputation. Together, these traits open doors for favor and good success—not through force or manipulation but through authentic integrity and consistent alignment with God’s will.

In conclusion, the love of God and dependability serve as pathways to favor and success, both with God and with others. They are qualities that not only honor God but also enhance a believer's influence, respect, and effectiveness in the world. Wisdom literature may not promise that every situation will turn out perfectly, yet the principle remains true: a life committed to God’s will often brings blessings and respect that enrich both the believer’s life and their community.
