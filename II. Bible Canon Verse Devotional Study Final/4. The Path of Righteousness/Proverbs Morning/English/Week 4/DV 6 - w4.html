Proverbs 6:6-8

NLT (New Living Translation)

6 "Go to the ant, O sluggard; consider her ways, and be wise. 7 Without having any chief, officer, or ruler, 8 she prepares her bread in summer and gathers her food in harvest.


GUIDE REFLECTION:


The Wisdom of the Ant: A Lesson Against Laziness (2/2)

Proverbs 6:7-8 teaches us a profound lesson about diligence and preparation, using the humble ant as an example. Solomon highlights how these tiny creatures work tirelessly without the need for a leader to command them. Their instinctive industriousness and foresight serve as a model for anyone striving to live wisely.

Ants exhibit a remarkable work ethic, laboring diligently to gather food during the summer and store it for the winter. Without external supervision or compulsion, they undertake their tasks with unwavering dedication. This self-motivation is a quality that Solomon commends, urging us to emulate the ant's example in our own lives.

As Christians, we are called to exhibit the same diligence and initiative in our work. The Apostle Paul echoes this principle:

Ephesians 6:6-7 ESV
6 not by the way of eye-service, as people-pleasers, but as bondservants of Christ, doing the will of God from the heart, 7 rendering service with a good will as to the Lord and not to man,

Whether in the workplace, at home, or in ministry, we should aim to perform our duties faithfullyâ€”not only to please others but to honor God. This commitment to excellence, even in the absence of supervision, reflects a heart aligned with God's purposes.

The ant's foresight is another critical lesson. By preparing during times of abundance, the ant ensures its survival during seasons of scarcity. This principle of preparation extends to every area of life.

Proverbs 10:5 NLT
A wise youth harvests in the summer, but one who sleeps during harvest is a disgrace.

Solomon underscores its importance again in Proverbs 10:5, where he contrasts the prudent son who gathers during the summer with the shameful son who neglects the harvest. Preparation is a mark of wisdom, and neglecting it often leads to hardship and regret.

Joseph, the biblical figure known for his wisdom, exemplifies this principle. When Pharaoh of Egypt had a dream predicting seven years of abundance followed by seven years of famine, Joseph advised him to store surplus grain during the plentiful years. This foresight not only saved Egypt from devastation but also provided for neighboring nations during the famine (Genesis 41). Joseph's story illustrates how God can use wisdom and preparation to bring about His plans and provide for His people.

In a spiritual sense, the ant's diligence reminds us of the importance of preparing for eternity. Just as the ant gathers food in the summer to survive the winter, we are called to use our time wisely to store up treasures in heaven.

Matthew 6:20 ESV
but lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven, where neither moth nor rust destroys and where thieves do not break in and steal.

This preparation involves cultivating our relationship with God, serving others, and living in obedience to His Word.

In conclusion, Proverbs 6:7-8 challenges us to learn from the ant's example of hard work, self-motivation, and preparation. By adopting these qualities, we not only honor God but also ensure that we are equipped to face life's challenges and contribute to the well-being of others. May we strive to work diligently and prepare wisely, knowing that our efforts reflect our faith and glorify the Lord.
