Proverbs 5:12

NLT (New Living Translation)

12 "You will say, 'How I hated discipline! If only I had not ignored all the warnings!"


GUIDE REFLECTION:


The High Cost of Ignoring Wisdom

Proverbs 5:12 vividly captures the sorrow of a person who failed to heed godly counsel: “You will say, ‘How I hated discipline! If only I had not ignored all the warnings!’” This lament embodies the regret of someone who has chosen sin over wisdom, only to suffer its painful consequences.

The allure of sin often lies in its promise of immediate gratification. Discipline, on the other hand, requires self-control and foresight—qualities that, while not always "fun," safeguard us from future regret. The momentary pleasure of yielding to temptation blinds individuals to the inevitable pain it brings: guilt and physical consequences.

Proverbs 5:11 NLT
In the end you will groan in anguish when disease consumes your body.

This verse also serves as a broader warning against disregarding God’s wisdom. Sin, in any form, thrives when we reject discipline and ignore the warnings of Scripture, mentors, and the Spirit. Those who despise correction often find themselves lamenting their choices too late. Guilt over past actions can be unbearable, especially when those actions bring irreversible consequences.

The story of Judas Iscariot illustrates this principle with chilling clarity. Judas ignored Jesus’ teachings and warnings, choosing instead to betray Him for thirty pieces of silver. His betrayal filled him with deep regret when he realized the gravity of his actions.

Matthew 27:3-4 ESV
3 Then when Judas, his betrayer, saw that Jesus was condemned, he changed his mind and brought back the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and the elders, 4 saying, “I have sinned by betraying innocent blood.” They said, “What is that to us? See to it yourself.”

Tragically, Judas allowed his remorse to lead to despair rather than to repentance. Instead of turning to God for forgiveness, he succumbed to hopelessness, ending his life in despair.

Matthew 27:5 ESV
And throwing down the pieces of silver into the temple, he departed, and he went and hanged himself.

This contrasts sharply with the example of Peter, who also sinned grievously by denying Jesus three times. Unlike Judas, Peter’s sorrow led him to godly repentance. He sought restoration and became a pillar of the early church.

Luke 22:61-62 ESV
61 And the Lord turned and looked at Peter. And Peter remembered the saying of the Lord, how he had said to him, “Before the rooster crows today, you will deny me three times.” 62 And he went out and wept bitterly.

This difference highlights the importance of responding to regret with humility and a willingness to return to God, rather than succumbing to hopelessness.

Proverbs 5:12 urges us to embrace discipline and heed warnings while there is still time. It reminds us that wisdom often requires choosing what is right over what is easy. As followers of Christ, we are called to submit to God’s guidance and resist the temptations that promise fleeting pleasure but bring lasting pain.

Ultimately, this verse challenges us to value discipline as a gift from God. It is through correction and instruction that we grow in wisdom and avoid the sorrow of regret.

Hebrews 12:11 ESV
For the moment all discipline seems painful rather than pleasant, but later it yields the peaceful fruit of righteousness to those who have been trained by it.

By trusting in God’s wisdom and submitting to His discipline, we can walk the path of life and avoid the lamentation of those who reject it.
