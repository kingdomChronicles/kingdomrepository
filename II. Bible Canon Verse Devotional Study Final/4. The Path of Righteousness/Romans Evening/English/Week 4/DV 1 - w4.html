Romans 1:18-25

ESV (English Standard Version)

18 For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness of men, who by their unrighteousness suppress the truth. 19 For what can be known about God is plain to them, because God has shown it to them. 20 For his invisible attributes, namely, his eternal power and divine nature, have been clearly perceived, ever since the creation of the world, in the things that have been made. So they are without excuse.21 For although they knew God, they did not honor him as God or give thanks to him, but they became futile in their thinking, and their foolish hearts were darkened. 22 Claiming to be wise, they became fools, 23 and exchanged the glory of the immortal God for images resembling mortal man and birds and animals and creeping things. 24 Therefore God gave them up in the lusts of their hearts to impurity, to the dishonoring of their bodies among themselves, 25 because they exchanged the truth about God for a lie and worshiped and served the creature rather than the Creator, who is blessed forever! Amen.

The Tragic Irony of False Wisdom

Romans 1:21 serves as a powerful reflection on the human condition when it comes to acknowledging God. The verse highlights humanity's deliberate rejection of God's authority and the devastating consequences that follow. It reads: "For although they knew God, they did not honor him as God or give thanks to him, but they became futile in their thinking, and their foolish hearts were darkened." This statement lays bare a sobering truth: humanity’s failure to honor and thank God stems not from ignorance of His existence but from a willful decision to deny Him.

Paul begins this chapter by asserting that God's wrath is revealed against the unrighteousness of humanity.

Romans 1:18 ESV
For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness of men, who by their unrighteousness suppress the truth.

This wrath is not arbitrary but justified because God has made Himself evident through creation. The intricate beauty and order of the world testify to His divine power and nature.

Romans 1:20 ESV
For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness of men, who by their unrighteousness suppress the truth.

Yet, humanity's response is one of dismissal, not reverence. Despite knowing God, people refuse to honor Him as Creator and Sustainer of life. This rejection is a deliberate choice, not a result of insufficient evidence.

Rejecting God as Creator has profound consequences. When we deny that God is the origin of all things, we lose the foundation for honoring Him. Without seeing Him as the provider of life and sustenance, gratitude fades away. Instead, humanity places confidence in its own abilities, crediting itself for achievements that ultimately depend on God. This prideful outlook leads to futile thinking—reasoning that is misguided and disconnected from ultimate truth. Without a right understanding of God, the entire framework of our thinking becomes flawed.

This futility of thought produces devastating outcomes. Darkened hearts—hearts that are blind to truth and hardened against God—are the inevitable result. When humanity refuses to acknowledge God, it becomes incapable of understanding life’s true purpose. This often leads to a worldview filled with hopelessness, as people begin to see life as meaningless. The denial of God as Creator and Sustainer leads to the rise of nihilism, the belief that life has no inherent value or purpose. Without God, the universe becomes an empty, chaotic place devoid of hope.

Romans 1:21 reminds us that turning away from God is not merely an intellectual mistake but a moral and spiritual failure. By rejecting Him, humanity drifts into darkness, losing the ability to perceive truth and purpose. This verse is both a warning and a call to recognize God for who He truly is—our Creator, Sustainer, and Redeemer. Only by honoring Him and giving thanks can we find the clarity, purpose, and hope that come from knowing and worshiping the one true God.
